# C14
_parent: **Substitute the Operator or Representation** (`architectural_operator_substitution`)_

## Tactical pattern

The cluster's move is to replace the dense O(N²) all-pairs pairwise-interaction operator — softmax self-attention — with a sub-quadratic surrogate while making the contribution the proof that the surrogate retains attention's load-bearing property rather than the raw FLOP reduction. Concrete surrogate constructions recur: factor the softmax kernel through (non-negative) randomized feature maps so its numerator and denominator collapse into cumulative sums evaluated by associativity; swap the dense interaction for depthwise large-kernel convolutions, bidirectional state-space scans, or implicitly parameterized long convolutions interleaved with multiplicative gating; or compress the interaction into structured factored linear maps (block-diagonal × permutation) or hierarchical/sparse routing graphs. A distinct second flavor proves an EXACT algebraic equivalence between attention and a primitive from an adjacent field — continuous Hopfield retrieval, a gated-RNN update, one gradient-descent step, outer-product memory — importing that field's guarantees and reinterpreting what attention computes. In every variant the deliverable is the preserved-property argument: expressive class, receptive-field size, retrieval capacity, or order-preservation.

## Step-by-Step

1. Localize the cost to one specific operator and write down explicitly the property it must preserve. Pin the bottleneck to a single object — the dense all-pairs interaction, an iterated propagation, a joint tensor product — and name the property whose loss would break the model: expressive class, receptive-field reach, retrieval capacity, or ordering sensitivity. Skipping this and treating the swap as self-evidently cheaper leaves no yardstick for fidelity and invites the 'rigorous but already believed' dismissal.
2. Choose a surrogate whose cheapness comes from an algebraic or structural lever, not from discarding inputs. Pick a primitive — kernel/random-feature factorization, convolution, state-space scan, structured factored matrix, hierarchical or sparse routing — whose lower cost follows from associativity, factorization, or compression so that no element is dropped from the interaction. Surrogates that buy speed by pruning, downsampling, or windowing away interactions tend to trade off the very property named in step 1.
3. Prove the surrogate preserves that property, surfacing the hidden invariant the fast computation secretly depends on. Either derive an approximation/equivalence bound or exhibit an exact algebraic identity, and explicitly identify and enforce the structural precondition the rewrite needs — positivity of the kernel, a low-rank-plus-normal decomposition, an order-preserving parameterization. A fidelity proof that never connects to a consequence, or a swap that silently violates the invariant, is the most common fatal gap.
4. Demonstrate that the substitution itself is the causal driver by controlling every confound. Hold fixed positional encodings, parameter count, added multi-scale or capacity features, and the training recipe so the measured gain cannot be attributed to anything but the operator swap, and benchmark not only against the expensive original but against the existing family of cheaper surrogates. Omitting the surrogate-family baselines makes the work read as a redundant reparameterization, and uncontrolled confounds make the headline conclusion unattributable.
5. Stress-test in the exact regime the substitution targets and state the consequence it unlocks. Evaluate at the scale or input length where the original operator fails — very long sequences, very large element sets, higher representational orders — and articulate what the swap delivers beyond efficiency: a previously infeasible task, a resolved efficiency-versus-quality tradeoff, or imported theory that reframes the operator. Small-scale-only validation, or a swap with no demonstrated downstream consequence, leaves the core promise untested.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster physically removes the operator and installs a cheaper primitive in its place, whereas sibling cluster 12 keeps the module intact and instead re-explains its mechanism — using Hessian curvature spectra to argue landscape flattening, not the conventionally attributed cause, drives generalization; that is a reinterpretation move, the opposite of a substitution. It also differs from sibling cluster 30, whose degree-parameterized reparameterization builds an infinite hierarchy of non-convex estimation problems to ease optimization/identifiability — a reparameterization of an estimation objective, not a cost-driven swap of a forward relational or sequence operator. The defining signature here is a sub-quadratic surrogate for the dense pairwise-interaction operator whose acceptance hinges on a preserved-capacity proof (expressivity, receptive field, retrieval capacity, or order-preservation), rather than on a new mechanistic explanation (cluster 12) or an estimation-tractability reparameterization (cluster 30).

## When to pick this one

Pick this instantiation when the bottleneck is concretely the dense pairwise-interaction (all-pairs / softmax-attention) operator over N elements and you can name a cheaper primitive whose preserved property — expressivity class, receptive field, or retrieval capacity — you can prove or exactly equate. Do not pick it when the operator's cost is acceptable but its mechanism is misunderstood (a re-explanation move) or when the expense lives in estimation/optimization rather than the forward interaction.

## Tactical failure mode

Papers in this cluster fail when the surrogate is offered as a cheaper-but-equivalent reparameterization with no load-bearing consequence: the preservation argument is absent, trivially true ('already believed by the field'), or proven yet never connected to a capability the original operator lacked. A second recurring shape is the unattributable gain — the operator swap co-varies with a confound (positional encoding, added parameters, multi-scale features, different pre-training data) and no controlled ablation isolates the substitution as the cause. A third is baseline myopia: comparing only against the expensive original while ignoring the established family of cheaper surrogates, so the contribution reads as a redundant re-derivation. These frequently combine with empirical thinness — validation only at small scale or short sequences, never stress-tested in the long-context or large-element regime the substitution is meant to rescue. A candidate author should self-check: have I proven the preserved property actually matters, ruled out every confound, beaten the existing surrogate family, and tested at the regime where the original operator breaks?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- When two failure modes are conventionally patched separately, showing they share a single root cause lets one operator substitution resolve both at once — a far stronger contribution than two independent fixes.
- A linear-time rewrite of a quadratic operator hinges on preserving a hidden algebraic invariant (such as kernel positivity); naming and enforcing that invariant, not the speedup itself, is the load-bearing insight.
- When an expressive operator resists efficient computation because of a localized numerical pathology, decompose it so the pathology sits in a small exactly-handled correction term and the remainder admits the fast algorithm.
- An exact (not approximate) algebraic equivalence between a popular operator and a primitive from an adjacent field retroactively grounds the operator and imports that field's decades of theory and guarantees.
- Recasting operator application in a second algebraic vocabulary can yield parameterizations — for instance order-preserving ones — that were impossible to express in the original framing, removing a cost that ordering constraints seemed to force.
- Treating an apparent cost-versus-capability tradeoff as a false dichotomy, then exhibiting a structured construction that achieves both simultaneously, is a high-yield reframing that resolves an open problem rather than merely approximating around it.

### Reject lessons
- If a confound (positional encoding, extra capacity, added multi-scale features) co-varies with your operator swap, the headline conclusion becomes unattributable and reviewers reject it as opportunistic.
- Comparing your cheaper operator only against the expensive original — never against the existing family of cheaper alternatives — makes it read as a redundant reparameterization rather than an advance.
- An equivalence or approximation proof with no downstream consequence is dismissed as formalizing what the field already assumed to be true.
- Bolting together established cheaper components without a single unifying load-bearing insight invites the 'combination of existing modules / engineering report' rejection.
- Failing to evaluate in the exact regime your substitution targets — long sequences, large scale, high representational order — leaves its core promise untested and the claim unverifiable.
- A contrived or artificially motivated setup, paired with ablations that fail to isolate the proposed mechanism, leaves the observed gains attributable to incidental added capacity rather than the substitution.
