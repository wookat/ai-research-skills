# C00
_parent: **Reframe as a Solvable Object** (`reframe_as_solvable_object`)_

## Tactical pattern

This cluster recasts an opaque transparency question about an already-trained model or dataset — which feature/datum/internal component is responsible, how uncertain a prediction is, what a hidden representation encodes — as a concrete measurement over a well-studied object: a manifold-constrained counterfactual optimization, a submodular subset selection, an expectation/estimator over marginal statistics, an additive decomposition into a (often sparse) basis, or an influence/Shapley score accumulated along the optimization trajectory. The decisive move is choosing the right unit of analysis and the right faithfulness constraint so the resulting scores reflect the model's actual computation rather than an off-distribution or post-hoc artifact — e.g., searching counterfactuals only on the learned data manifold, decomposing into monosemantic sparse directions before causal tracing, or embedding the explanatory predictors as the primary decision representation. Tractability is then bought with a single compression trick (random projection, greedy submodular search, fixed-size accumulator vectors, closed-form per-step expansion). Fidelity is finally certified either by a causal intervention that flips model behavior when the identified object is perturbed, or by a soundness/optimality proof, rather than by the visual plausibility of the produced explanation.

## Step-by-Step

1. Pin down the opaque target as a precise quantity. Take the informal question — why did it decide this, how much did this datum matter, how confident should it be — and commit to a formal definition of responsibility or uncertainty rather than leaving it implicit. A definition you cannot defend as the 'real' quantity (no soundness argument, no axiomatic grounding) is the first thing reviewers attack, so make the definition itself a load-bearing claim, not a notational convenience.
2. Map the quantity onto an existing well-understood object and its solver. Recognize the target as an instance of counterfactual optimization, subset selection, expectation estimation, basis decomposition, or influence/Shapley allocation so that mature machinery and guarantees apply. Choose the object whose native correctness criterion already coincides with what 'faithful' means for your quantity, rather than the one that is merely most familiar.
3. Fix the unit of analysis and the faithfulness constraint before computing anything. Decide whether the atoms are raw inputs, grouped regions, native architectural components, or a learned sparse basis, and impose the constraint that keeps every output on the model's own manifold or consistent with its computation. Skipping this — pointing an off-the-shelf tool at a coarser granularity or a new architecture without re-justifying that the signal survives the move — is the canonical mechanical-wrapper rejection.
4. Buy tractability with one compression or linearization trick. Convert the exact-but-expensive object (exponential subset re-evaluation, full-parameter kernels, trajectory re-simulation) into something computable via random projection, greedy submodular search, fixed-size accumulators, or local per-step expansion. The trick must provably preserve the ranking or value it approximates; a fast approximation that silently changes the quantity is no better than the heuristic it replaces.
5. Certify fidelity causally or by proof, not by plausibility. Validate that perturbing or ablating the identified object actually shifts model behavior in the predicted direction, or prove the estimator is unbiased, optimal, or sound under stated conditions. Correlational evidence, eyeballed heatmaps, or 'looks reasonable' without a confound-controlling test is precisely the gap that sinks otherwise-clever reframings.

## Differentiation within parent

Where sibling cluster 27 reframes its target as a differentiable multi-agent game whose Nash equilibrium is engineered to coincide with the desired solution — making equilibrium existence/coincidence the correctness criterion — this cluster reframes opaque model-transparency questions as a measurement: an estimator, selector, or decomposition whose output is a per-unit responsibility or uncertainty score and whose correctness criterion is fidelity-to-a-fixed-model, certified by causal intervention or a soundness proof. Sibling cluster 21 instead reframes its problem as a generative iterated-transmission / emergent-language process — a dynamics over successive learner generations rather than a static scoring of one trained artifact. The distinguishing move here is that the object being solved produces an attribution or uncertainty number about an already-trained model or dataset and is validated by perturbing that model, not by reaching an equilibrium (cluster 27) or simulating a transmission dynamic (cluster 21).

## When to pick this one

Pick this instantiation when the problem is to explain, attribute, or quantify confidence about a single already-trained model or fixed dataset and you need a faithful per-unit responsibility or uncertainty score — not when the structure is competitive/multi-party (which favors an equilibrium reframe) or generative across successive populations. The tell is a fixed artifact whose internal behavior you must measure, plus an existing measurement object (counterfactual, subset selection, decomposition, influence/Shapley, causal mediation) you can validate by perturbing the model itself.

## Tactical failure mode

The signature failure is treating the reframing as a mechanical wrapper: taking an established attribution, decomposition, or estimation tool and pointing it at a new input granularity, a new architecture, or a new domain without re-deriving why the resulting scores are faithful to the model. These papers supply the computation but not the justification — no causal validation that the identified components actually drive behavior (only correlation), no axiomatic or soundness argument for why the proposed definition is the 'real' importance, and no confound control, so a reviewer can point to a methodological artifact (a computation-granularity difference, a degenerate zero baseline, an off-manifold perturbation) that explains the result instead. A second variant under-specifies the object itself — an unformalized threat model, an operationally undefined key concept, or a fitting choice that makes all contributions trivially equal — leaving the central claim unverifiable. A third variant surveys or recombines existing tools without a unifying axis or a decisive experiment, so the contribution reads as community-local rather than novel. The common root is the assumption that because the target object is well-studied, faithfulness and novelty transfer for free; they do not — the reframing's value lives entirely in the faithfulness constraint and the validation, not in the act of mapping.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Embedding the explanatory quantities as the primary basis of the model's decision — rather than fitting them as a separate post-hoc proxy — makes an explanation sound by construction, even when it introduces a circular dependency you must resolve with lagged targets.
- Constraining a counterfactual search or decomposition to the model's own learned geometry (a generative manifold, a residual-additive subspace, a shared cross-modal space) keeps the reframed object faithful instead of producing off-distribution artifacts.
- Abandoning the assumption that attribution must cover the entire input lets you reframe explanation as compact subset selection and inherit a provable near-optimality guarantee from submodularity.
- An elegant but exponentially expensive attribution definition becomes a usable tool only after a single compression move — random projection, fixed-size trajectory accumulators, or a local per-step expansion — collapses it to a cheap lookup or closed form.
- Pairing a discovered internal structure with a causal intervention that flips behavior when it is perturbed converts correlational interpretability into causal evidence reviewers trust.
- When the quantity you want is provably simpler than the model that produces it, estimate it directly from available marginal statistics and skip the model-fitting detour entirely.

### Reject lessons
- Pointing an off-the-shelf decomposition at a coarser input granularity without showing the coarser units preserve the signal reads as a wrapper, not a contribution.
- Porting a known attribution technique to a new architecture or domain while ignoring that technique's documented failure modes — and omitting comparisons to alternatives — leaves reviewers unable to see any unique benefit.
- Proposing a new attribution definition without an axiomatic or causal argument for why it should count as ground-truth collapses the moment a reviewer asks why this is the real importance.
- Conflating a methodological artifact — a computation-granularity difference, a degenerate zero baseline — with genuine signal undermines the entire premise once the confound is identified.
- Building a suppression or privacy method without formalizing the threat model leaves the central guarantee unverifiable regardless of strong empirical numbers.
- Surveying or recombining fragmented methods without a unifying axis or a decisive controlled experiment yields a contribution reviewers dismiss as anecdotal.
