# C12
_parent: **Substitute the Operator or Representation** (`architectural_operator_substitution`)_

## Tactical pattern

Within a deep, multi-stage compositional architecture (layered network, stacked blocks, probabilistic circuit), this cluster relocates or swaps a *structural slot* rather than tuning weights: it moves where trainable parameters live (from nodes to inter-unit connections; from stored interaction scalars to coordinate geometry), rewires the inter-stage routing topology (skip-connection target redirected from the adjacent stage to the final aggregation point; carry-forward state pulled from two stages back; prior-unit outputs consumed as first-class inputs), or substitutes the per-stage aggregation/representation primitive (full summation → max/min extremum selection; closed-form density → bijectively equivalent characteristic function; factored dimension-matched determinant → a single overparametrized Pfaffian of arbitrary rank; deep forward passes → role-partitioned lookup tables). The substitute is repeatedly anchored to a classical theorem or a revived classical representation that fits the same slot, and the move is accompanied by a proof or a curvature/gradient-dynamics measurement showing the essential property (expressivity, required symmetry, gradient flow, capacity equivalence) survives while a cost/expressivity property changes.

## Step-by-Step

1. Locate the cost or expressivity bottleneck inside the multi-stage pipeline and pin it to one concrete structural degree of freedom — the *placement* of trainable parameters, the *wiring* between stages, or the *primitive operator* that aggregates or represents at each stage. Name the exact element you intend to move or swap; pointing only at 'the architecture is expensive' or 'this module over-generalizes' reads as an undifferentiated tweak and gives reviewers nothing to evaluate as a contribution.
2. Identify a surrogate that can occupy the same structural slot while changing the targeted property — ideally one grounded in a classical theorem, a bijectively equivalent representation, or a revived classical construction that the field had dismissed or never connected to this slot. Re-derive why the surrogate fits *this* problem's structure; a surrogate lifted wholesale from a separate application domain, with a coupling mechanism bolted on, will be read as borrowing rather than insight unless its fit to the target task is argued from first principles.
3. Prove or directly measure that the substitution preserves the essential property the architecture relied on — universal approximation, a required symmetry or constraint, stable gradient flow, or an exact capacity/representational equivalence between old and new slot. The preservation argument must reveal new structure: a proof that is a trivial adaptation of an existing result, or that establishes a property orthogonal to the one the swap was meant to buy, leaves the load-bearing claim unsupported.
4. Demonstrate that the surrogate not only matches the original but *unlocks something the original could not* — a strictly larger object class, configuration-agnostic generalization, symbolic extractability, emergent compression, or a property (parallelism, constant-time inference, manifold faithfulness) that the original slot structurally forbids. Showing mere parity, or a marginal metric gain at added cost, collapses the work into an incremental variant of the architecture it replaces.
5. Validate the unlocked gain in the exact regime and at the scale where it is claimed, against baselines matched to that specific property. Reporting a secondary proxy — throughput without accuracy, toy-scale data, or no matched-compression/matched-parameter comparison — while leaving the headline property unverified is the single most common reason an otherwise-sound substitution is rejected.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster targets the macro-structural plumbing of deep compositional networks — parameter locus, skip/routing topology, and per-stage aggregation primitive — whereas sibling cluster 14 stays *inside* a single interaction score, disentangling it into factored content×position cross-product terms; cluster 12 papers rewire how stages connect and where parameters sit rather than decomposing one pairwise scoring function. Against cluster 30, which reparameterizes a non-convex *estimation/optimization* problem with a degree-indexed hierarchy to reshape the loss landscape, cluster 12's substitutions act on the forward computation graph itself (which operator aggregates, which connection carries the signal) and keep the network end-to-end trainable, not on the geometry of an estimator's objective. And unlike cluster 9, which reformulates *certification* by shifting from post-hoc artifact inspection to procedure attestation, cluster 12 never touches verification — it swaps the building blocks that produce the forward output.

## When to pick this one

Pick this when the bottleneck lives in a staged network's structural plumbing — where trainable parameters sit, how stages connect, or what operator aggregates per stage — and you can name a classical-theorem-grounded or bijectively-equivalent surrogate that fits that exact slot while flipping a cost or expressivity property. Choose a scoring-function decomposition (14) instead if the bottleneck is inside a single interaction term, or an estimator reparameterization (30) if it is in the optimization landscape rather than the forward graph.

## Tactical failure mode

The recurring failure is a substitution that is mechanically valid but whose payoff is absent or unverified. Authors successfully swap the operator, wiring, or parameter-locus, then stall in one of three ways: they prove preservation with a borrowed or trivial argument that exposes no new structure; they claim the property the new slot was supposed to buy — parallelism, aggressive pruning, scalability — without actually delivering or measuring it; or they port an architecture from another domain and add a coupling mechanism without grounding it in the target problem, producing an uncoordinated assembly rather than an insight. A diagnostic tell is validation on a proxy: throughput reported instead of accuracy, toy-scale data, or no matched baseline for the specific property claimed. Self-check: can you exhibit at least one object, capability, or regime the original structure provably cannot reach, and is the property the swap buys measured head-on rather than assumed to follow from the swap?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Reviving a classical decomposition theorem the field dismissed as impractical — and showing the dismissal was an artifact of its minimal (shallow) form rather than the theorem itself — is among the highest-yield substitution moves.
- Replacing a factored, dimension-matched constraint operator with a single overparametrized operator of arbitrary rank decouples parameter count from input configuration and converts a hand-crafted per-instance selection procedure into a fully end-to-end learnable one.
- An over-parameterized, distributed per-unit representation can be more *learnable* than the minimal parsimonious one, because neural feature processing couples more tightly to local unit-level targets than to a single compact global tuple.
- Treating the destination of a skip connection as a tunable degree of freedom — not a fixed adjacent-stage convention — lets you reorder backward gradient magnitudes and induce emergent structural compression with no auxiliary objective.
- Substituting one representation for a bijectively equivalent one keeps the compositional structure fully intact while unlocking a strictly larger class of objects that had no usable form in the original representation.
- Recasting an established operator through a frequency or curvature lens and grounding its benefit in measurable optimization geometry can falsify the field's prevailing mechanistic story and directly predict a better hybrid placement.

### Reject lessons
- Proving the substitute operator preserves universal approximation via a trivial adaptation of known proofs, while never connecting that result to the practical advantage (e.g. sparsification) that motivated the swap, makes the contribution read as incremental.
- Claiming the rewired structure delivers a systems property — such as parallel forward/backward execution — that the underlying algorithm does not actually provide invites reviewers to falsify the headline claim head-on.
- A training-time re-parameterization that yields only marginal accuracy gains at higher training cost, without strong matched-baseline comparisons, reads as added overhead rather than as an insight.
- An elegant geometric reanalysis tool validated only on toy data, with no quantitative metric and an unclear delta over existing sensitivity measures, fails to show the substitution buys anything new.
- Rewiring the dependency chain and reporting only throughput/speedup while omitting accuracy validation at scale leaves the essential preserved-property claim unverified.
- Porting a two-component architecture from a separate domain and bolting on a cross-pathway coupling, without a unifying contribution grounded in the target task, reads as an uncoordinated assembly rather than a substitution insight.
