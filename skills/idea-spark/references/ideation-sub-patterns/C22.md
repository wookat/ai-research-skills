## Tactical pattern

The shared move is to reify a previously hand-engineered design artifact — a network architecture, a training objective, a fine-tuning recipe, an agent/workflow topology, a skill set, or an adaptation strategy — as a first-class search variable inside a single unified, shared-parameter configuration space, then collapse the conventional search-then-retrain hierarchy. Concretely: encode the heterogeneous discrete variants into one parameterization (computation graph, hierarchical genome, weight-sharing supernet, executable-code representation, typed symbolic program, or continuous mask variables), share parameters/weights across all candidates so structure and weights co-optimize in one pass, and frequently make the chosen configuration instance- or context-conditioned (a routing function or learned distribution over configs) rather than a single global point solution. The verbs are "encode-into-one-parameterization, share-weights-across-candidates, jointly-optimize-topology-with-task-loss, condition-config-on-instance" — not merely "relax and optimize." The differentiator from the parent's generic signature is the target (a thing humans used to design by hand) plus the unification artifact that subsumes the discrete variants.

## Step-by-Step

1. Name the hand-engineered artifact the field currently treats as fixed scaffolding — the architecture, the objective, the inter-module wiring, the adaptation recipe — and declare it a search variable rather than a designer's prerogative. The reframe itself is part of the contribution (e.g., recasting an undesired output property as an architectural property, or treating fine-tuning placement as searchable). Boundary: if the field already searches this exact artifact with a standard reduction, the act of searching is not novel; you must bring a representational or hierarchy-collapsing insight, not just a new target.
2. Construct a single unified parameterization that subsumes the discrete variants — a graph, genome, weight-sharing supernet, executable-code representation, or symbolic program — chosen so the space is simultaneously expressive (it contains configurations meaningfully different from known ones) and well-conditioned (candidates instantiate stably without numerical pathology). Ground the encoding in a unifying formulation or theory of the component family where possible. Boundary: an ad-hoc combinatorial grid that merely enumerates component swaps reads as 'a known search technique on a new target' and invites a novelty rejection.
3. Collapse the nested search-then-train loop into one joint optimization: share weights across candidates, embed structural decisions as differentiable masks or as early-sequence decisions in a single trajectory, or amortize the search entirely through a learned instance-to-configuration predictor or generator. The goal is that a single pass replaces per-candidate retraining or independent performance estimators. Boundary: when weight-sharing couples configuration choice to a context that changes what each candidate must compute, naive sharing produces a biased surrogate — partition or condition the estimator so performance prediction reflects the right context.
4. Where instance heterogeneity is the real lever, make the configuration instance- or context-conditioned: learn a distribution or routing function over configurations and defer or adapt the final choice at deployment, rather than committing to one global optimum. Boundary: a one-size-fits-all point solution leaves the efficiency and adaptivity gains unrealized and reads as incremental; conversely, per-instance from-scratch search reintroduces the very cost the amortization was meant to remove.
5. Validate with evidence a reviewer cannot reduce to engineering: rediscover a known solution inside the search space as a sanity check, beat strong hand-designed baselines across diverse benchmarks, or surface an emergent/surprising configuration — and position explicitly against the closest amortization, transfer, and compression neighbors. Boundary: single-run numbers, toy-scale settings, efficiency claimed only on a weak proxy, or a missing comparison to the nearest established alternative collapses the whole contribution back to 'we automated X.'

## When to pick this one

Instantiate the parent as this cluster when the discrete object you want to search is itself a previously hand-engineered design decision (an architecture, objective, recipe, or topology) and many such configurations can plausibly share one parameterization or be predicted from the instance — i.e., the bottleneck is repeated manual design plus per-candidate retraining, not a single one-off combinatorial solve.

## Tactical failure mode

Reject papers here typically execute the reification move correctly — they do turn a hand-designed artifact into a searchable space — but reach for an off-the-shelf search apparatus (DAG traversal, MDP plus dynamic programming, RL over a combinatorial action set, or supervised regression from instance to configuration), so the work reads as a known technique pointed at a new target rather than a representational insight. The contribution collapses to the act of searching or automating, with no unification or hierarchy-collapse that was previously impossible; reviewers call this brute-force or incremental. Compounding this, validation is thin: single-run results, toy-scale benchmarks, efficiency reported on one weak proxy (e.g., parameter count where it is known to mislead), and — most damaging — no positioning against the closely adjacent amortization, transfer, or compression literature, so the practical advantage over established neighbors is never demonstrated. The structural self-check: if deleting your custom representation leaves a textbook search loop any practitioner would write, and your benchmarks are small with a single seed and no nearest-neighbor comparison, you are in this failure shape.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Encoding the searched object as a typed symbolic program buys interpretability, warm-starting from known solutions, and cross-domain transfer at once — and rediscovering an established solution inside your own search space is the most disarming validation possible.
- Inverting the search direction — growing structure from a minimal seed instead of pruning an over-parameterized supernet — can make the search itself cheaper than a single instance of the thing being searched.
- Collapsing a nested design-then-evaluate hierarchy into early-sequence decisions within one optimization trajectory lets a single gradient update improve both the structure and how it is used, eliminating the need for per-candidate performance estimators.
- Grounding the search-space encoding in a unifying mathematical theory of the component family is what simultaneously keeps candidates numerically stable and the space genuinely expressive; an ad-hoc combinatorial space delivers neither.
- Treat instance heterogeneity as the primary lever rather than noise: learn a distribution or routing function over configurations and defer the final choice to deployment instead of searching for one global point solution.
- Reframing an undesired property the field blamed on the data as a property of the architecture converts a saturated remediation problem into an open search problem with Pareto-dominant solutions.

### Reject lessons
- If deleting your custom representation leaves a textbook search loop (DAG traversal, MDP plus dynamic programming, RL over an action set), reviewers will score the contribution as a known technique applied to a new target.
- Automating or searching a design artifact is not by itself a contribution; absent a representational or hierarchy-collapsing insight, the work reads as brute-force even when it works.
- Single-run results on toy-scale benchmarks let reviewers dismiss genuine gains as unverified — multi-seed, at-scale validation is the price of entry at a top venue.
- Failing to position against the closest amortization, transfer, and compression neighbors leaves the practical advantage unproven, which is fatal even when the core idea is novel.
- Claiming efficiency on a single proxy invites rejection when that proxy is known to be poor for the target structure; report the metric that actually binds, such as FLOPs, latency, or peak memory.
- Incremental modifications over a prior search method fail the novelty bar when they amount to hyperparameter changes describable in a few lines, regardless of competitive numbers.
