# C09
_parent: **Substitute the Operator or Representation** (`architectural_operator_substitution`)_

## Tactical pattern

Insert a low-sensitivity intermediate object — a linear sketch, a partitioned binary accumulator, a hierarchically-separated tree, a matrix decomposition, a low-rank projection, or a calibrated input-space transform — between the sensitive raw input and the downstream procedure, then attach the privacy/perturbation (or third-party verification) mechanism to that intermediate rather than to the raw input or the final output. The load-bearing artifact is a per-element influence/sensitivity bound on the intermediate: each record toggles a single bit, modifies only a bounded subset of components, degrades the privacy parameter by just √m, or contributes only a gap-normalized residual. That bound proves the noise (or verification) budget no longer scales with the ambient quantity — output dimension, procedure runtime, or solution-set cardinality — that made the naive mechanism intractable. Utility is then shown to match the best unconstrained/non-private baseline for the same task, frequently paired with a matching lower bound that upgrades "cheaper mechanism" to "provably optimal mechanism."

## Step-by-Step

1. Locate the layer where the costly mechanism currently attaches. The noise injection, perturbation, or verification is typically bolted onto the raw input or the final structured output, and its cost scales with an ambient quantity — dimensionality, runtime, or number of feasible solutions. Recognize this scaling as an artifact of where the mechanism sits, not an intrinsic property of the problem.
2. Find or construct an intermediate representation with provably bounded per-element influence. Look for a compact object the computation already routes through — a sketch, summary, partition, decomposition, or projection — such that any single input element touches only a small, fixed portion of it. The whole tactic lives or dies on this object existing and its influence being literally bounded (one bit, a fixed-size subset, a √m factor), not merely 'small in practice'.
3. Relocate the mechanism onto the intermediate and prove the influence bound as a new structural fact. The bound must be something a re-analysis of the incumbent method would not already yield — a fixed input-independent decomposition, a local leave-one-group-out privacy lemma, a recursive residual-tightening loop. Do not stop at composing two off-the-shelf primitives; a moment-reformulation stacked on a random projection, with no fresh sensitivity insight, reads as 'not a contribution in itself'.
4. Transfer the essential property end-to-end and quantify the gap over the substituted-out approach. Prove the privacy/verification guarantee carries from the intermediate back to the original problem AND that utility matches the strongest unconstrained baseline, ideally with a matching lower bound. A gain that is marginal, or holds only in a narrow regime where existing analyses might match it, will be judged a non-improvement.
5. Instantiate on concrete tasks with airtight internal consistency and a thorough prior-art comparison. Make the claimed bounds in the abstract identical to the proven bounds in the theorems, state every assumption the correctness rests on, and benchmark against the best existing method rather than a strawman. Shaky assumptions, an unverified correctness appeal, or a missing comparison sink the result regardless of how elegant the relocation is.

## Differentiation within parent

All four clusters substitute a representation, but what the substitution is chosen to preserve differs sharply. Here the surrogate is selected for its bounded per-element influence, and the essential property preserved is a formal privacy/confidentiality/adaptive-correctness guarantee while utility is held at the non-private baseline — the noise or verification mechanism is physically relocated onto the low-sensitivity intermediate. Sibling cluster 14 substitutes representation to preserve and expand the expressivity of pairwise interactions (splitting a unit into content and position vectors and re-deriving the interaction score), where sensitivity to individual records is irrelevant. Sibling cluster 30 reparameterizes a non-convex factored formulation to preserve the optimum while reshaping the optimization landscape, a move aimed at trainability rather than disclosure cost. The distinguishing signature of cluster 9 is that the surrogate's selling point is a provable cap on how much one input element can move it, and the payoff is a privacy-utility (or verification-cost) frontier, not an expressivity or optimization-landscape gain.

## When to pick this one

Pick this when the bottleneck is a per-element sensitivity, disclosure, or verification cost that scales with ambient dimension, runtime, or solution-set size, and there is (or can be built) a compact object the computation routes through whose per-element influence is provably bounded. The tell is a formal privacy/confidentiality/adaptive-adversary constraint where the cost is an artifact of attaching the mechanism to the raw input or final output rather than to that intermediate.

## Tactical failure mode

The recurring failure is that the substitution is performed but the gap over the substituted-out baseline is never convincingly established. One shape is the mechanical composition: the intermediate is just two pre-existing primitives stacked together (a moment reformulation feeding a random projection), yielding no new sensitivity bound, so reviewers see 'an obvious idea' and 'not enough of a contribution in itself.' A second shape is the marginal or regime-bound gain: the relocated mechanism improves the approximation ratio or additive error only slightly, or only in a narrow corner (small diameter), where it is unclear whether re-analyzing the incumbent method would match it. A third shape is the unsound or sloppy proof: the privacy/correctness argument leans on an assumption that may not hold (congeniality) or an under-justified theorem appeal, or the paper states one bound in the abstract and proves a weaker one, with no thorough comparison to a heavily-studied prior literature. To self-check: can you exhibit an influence/sensitivity bound that is genuinely new — one no re-analysis of the existing approach would produce — and is the resulting privacy-utility gap quantified, internally consistent, assumption-clean, and benchmarked against the strongest prior art?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- The highest-yield move is relocating the noise or perturbation from the raw input or final output onto an intermediate representation whose per-element influence is provably bounded.
- A less space-efficient structure can be strictly superior for privacy: compression that shrinks memory often raises per-element influence and thereby worsens the privacy-accuracy tradeoff.
- When efficient non-private algorithms already route through compact, sparse, bounded-range intermediates, privatize those intermediates directly instead of designing new approximation algorithms from scratch.
- Encoding the entire indistinguishability budget into a calibrated pre-processing transform of the input lets an unmodified black-box solver inherit the guarantee at zero runtime overhead.
- Decoupling the property to be certified (the constraint) from the quality metric (the objective) lets an external party attest only the constraint, collapsing verification cost from linear to sublinear.
- Pairing a sensitivity-reduced upper bound with a matching lower bound converts 'a cheaper mechanism' into 'a provably optimal mechanism,' which is what elevates the contribution.

### Reject lessons
- A substitution that is merely two known primitives composed, carrying no new sensitivity bound, reads as 'not enough of a contribution in and of itself.'
- An improvement that holds only in a narrow regime and might be matched by re-analyzing existing methods will be judged a non-improvement, not a contribution.
- Porting an aggregation rule across settings collapses when its correctness rests on an assumption that may not hold and the key theorem appeal is not rigorously nailed down.
- An internal inconsistency between the bound claimed in the abstract and the bound actually proven, plus a missing thorough prior-art comparison, sinks the work regardless of the idea's merit.
