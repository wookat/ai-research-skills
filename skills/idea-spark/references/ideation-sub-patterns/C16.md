# C16
_parent: **Encode Structure by Construction** (`structural_prior_encoding`)_

## Tactical pattern

Identify the symmetry group that genuinely acts on the problem's data or parameter space — a continuous geometric group (SE(3)/SO(3), scale, gauge), a discrete group (permutation of interchangeable units, sign-alternation), or an algebraic group (Clifford/geometric-algebra, group-commutant) — and then either (a) construct operators that commute with the group action exactly by construction (equivariant message-passing in raw coordinate space, group-averaging over a structured representative frame, equivariant diffusion transition kernels that induce invariant distributions, or a complete basis of equivariant linear maps read off an algebraic duality), or (b) characterize and complete a symmetry group the field has only partially recognized and exploit it for alignment/merging. The load-bearing verbs are derive, enumerate, lift, average, commute, and characterize; the objects are group actions, equivariant operators, frames, transition kernels, commutant bases, and permutation/scaling reparameterizations. The defining commitment is that the invariant/equivariant property is certified analytically rather than approximated by augmentation or learned from data.

## Step-by-Step

1. Pin down the exact symmetry group acting on your data or parameter space, and separate the true effective group from the naive one. Over-claiming the full group when the data only lives on a subgroup orbit needlessly forfeits expressivity, while under-claiming when a richer (often continuous) group is hiding leaves correctness or merging gains on the table — the effective group is itself a non-trivial object to get right.
2. Choose your route: construct operators that commute with the group by construction, or characterize/complete a symmetry group the field has only partially mapped. Either way, account for the admissible maps completely or prove sufficiency. A construction that silently restricts or relaxes the operator space without a completeness argument reads to reviewers as a 'simple variation of standard techniques' rather than a contribution.
3. Derive the operator family analytically — read equivariant maps off an algebraic duality, average a base procedure over a structured representative subset, prove an equivariant transition operator induces an invariant distribution, or lift the group action to an algebra automorphism. The derivation, not the network wiring, is the load-bearing artifact; a theorem with no instantiable operator collapses into 'mathematics in search of an application.'
4. Instantiate the operators as a working module and bind the symmetry to a concrete payoff a generic model cannot obtain for free: state-of-the-art on a geometric task, near-zero-barrier interpolation between independently trained solutions, lossless state-space compression, or sharp sample efficiency. A unified or general symmetry framework that merely matches specialized baselines — rather than beating at least one — converts versatility into a liability.
5. Preserve every property the downstream task depends on while enforcing the symmetry, and verify the construction introduces no defect the guarantee would otherwise mask — broken differentiability, discarded directional signal, or quantization artifacts. Demonstrate the failure of the un-encoded baseline precisely in the regime where the symmetry bites (long iterative rollouts, large instance sizes, off-manifold or arbitrarily-oriented inputs), so the inductive bias is shown to be necessary, not decorative.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster's signature is a group-theoretic equivariance/invariance certificate: a mathematically known symmetry group is baked into operators (or completed and exploited) so the property holds by construction and is never relearned. Sibling cluster 13 instead LEARNS relational structure from data — it augments a learned pairwise interaction-weighting mechanism with a self-supervised relational-link objective, so the structure is discovered and scored rather than derived from a known group; that is the opposite of this cluster's analytic-derivation commitment. Sibling cluster 20 is not about symmetry at all — it aligns a corrupted learner's internal salience/routing distributions to a clean reference for robustness, a representation-matching tactic with no group action in play. Where 13 supplies missing structure through auxiliary supervision and 20 repairs internal routing, this cluster's distinctive move is enforcing or enumerating an exact algebraic symmetry.

## When to pick this one

Pick this instantiation when the problem carries a mathematically characterizable symmetry group — rotation/translation, permutation of interchangeable units, scaling from operator homogeneity, or an algebraic group — whose orbit the data or parameters genuinely traverse, and you can derive the equivariant operators or enumerate the group exactly. If the relevant structure must instead be discovered empirically or supplied by auxiliary supervision, you are in a sibling cluster, not this one.

## Tactical failure mode

With seven Reject papers this is an adequate sample, and the recurring failure shape is consistent. The most common structural flaw is presenting a symmetry construction that is a special case or simple variation of existing equivariant machinery whose operator basis is never shown to be complete — once a relaxation to a subgroup or a low-order restriction silently drops part of the admissible map space, reviewers read it as expressivity-limiting rather than novel. A second shape is the correct-but-unmotivated result: propositions without proofs, no real downstream application, and a narrow architecture or single contrived experiment, so the work reads as 'mathematics in search of a problem.' A third is the unified symmetry-invariant representation that generalizes across many tasks from one set of parameters yet beats no specialized baseline on any of them, turning versatility into a weakness. A fourth is the symmetry-enforcing shortcut that silently breaks a property the task depends on (smoothness/differentiability, directional signal, quantization-free coordinates) even while being faster and provably symmetric. Self-check: Is my operator family proven complete or provably sufficient? Does the symmetry buy a concrete advantage a generic model cannot get for free? Does it beat — not merely match — specialized baselines? And does enforcing it break anything the downstream objective needs?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- A tiny structured subset of an intractable transformation family — chosen so the subset itself satisfies a subset-level symmetry property — can replace averaging over the whole family, collapsing the exactness-versus-tractability tradeoff the field had accepted as unavoidable.
- Shifting the invariance burden from the representation to the operator — proving an equivariant transition kernel preserves an invariant distribution — lets you model the raw, non-invariant representation directly instead of routing through lossy hand-crafted invariant features.
- Reading the complete equivariant operator basis off an algebraic duality (a commutant algebra, a conjugation automorphism that preserves the multiplicative law) eliminates the costly irreducible-decomposition detour everyone treated as mandatory.
- Discovering that the field has characterized only part of a parameterization's symmetry group — continuous maps beyond discrete reorderings, scaling symmetries from operator homogeneity, joint module-and-selector permutations — and then completing it is among the highest-yield moves available.
- When an external force or boundary condition breaks full symmetry, the correct inductive bias is the exact effective subgroup, not the full group; imposing too much symmetry is as wrong as imposing too little.
- Symmetry need not be wired into each architecture — it can emerge at the aggregate of an ensemble under full orbit coverage, or be measured post hoc with a differential operator — reframing equivariance from a hard construction constraint into a controllable, auditable quantity.

### Reject lessons
- A layer that relaxes a global symmetry constraint to a subgroup may gain expressivity on paper, but without a proof that the resulting operator family is complete it is dismissed as a special case of known techniques.
- A correct and novel group-theoretic result presented without proofs and without a developed real-world application reads as 'mathematics in search of a problem,' no matter how clean the algebra.
- A unified symmetry-invariant representation that serves many tasks from a single set of parameters but fails to beat any specialized baseline on any task turns its own versatility into the decisive weakness.
- A symmetry-enforcing shortcut that breaks a property the downstream objective silently depends on — differentiability, smoothness, directional signal — gets rejected even when it is faster and provably respects the target symmetry.
- Symmetry-exploiting augmentation that requires bespoke per-problem symmetry analysis is read as narrow engineering rather than a transferable method, capping its perceived generality.
