# C26
_parent: **Manufacture the Supervisory Signal** (`self_supervised_signal_engineering`)_

## Tactical pattern

This cluster manufactures a comparative or scalar reward signal — pairwise preference pairs, typed dense rewards, or quality scores — from a non-human source, then optimizes the target model against it with a contrastive/preference objective (DPO-style ranking, reward-model backprop, or score-conditioning) rather than label-fitting. The recurring moves are concrete: extract preference pairs from structures that already encode an ordering (pruned search-tree branches as dispreferred against the surviving path, the policy's own internal output scores, strong-versus-weak model contrasts, execution-verified versus failed trajectories); re-granularize the comparison to match where credit assignment fails (a single end-of-sequence scalar → step-level or error-typed signals); and convert noisy absolute scores into reliable relative orderings via pairwise ranking loss. The signal source is a learned reward model, an AI/oracle judge, or the policy itself, and the optimization treats the comparison — not a point label — as the unit of supervision. Several entries push further by embedding the preference signal into the model's native training objective so alignment becomes a special case of pretraining rather than a grafted-on correction.

## Step-by-Step

1. Diagnose the missing supervision as comparative rather than categorical: confirm you can rank or score outputs by quality even though you cannot assign each a single ground-truth label. If the real gap is just 'no labels,' a pseudo-label-and-filter loop fits better; reserve this tactic for when a preference ordering or reward score is the natural unit of signal. Resist stopping at a benchmark or dataset that merely measures the gap — the signal must be built to be consumed by an optimizer.
2. Locate a non-human source that already encodes the comparison and harvest it: pruned branches of a search tree as dispreferred against the surviving path, the policy's own internal output scores, a strong-versus-weak model contrast, or execution-verified versus failed trajectories. Prefer sources whose orderings are in-distribution for the model being trained; externally curated or stronger-model labels are not automatically superior and can underperform self-generated in-distribution comparisons.
3. Set the comparison granularity to match where credit assignment fails. When a single end-of-output scalar cannot localize which step or which quality dimension caused the failure, decompose into step-level or error-typed signals so the gradient points at the responsible locus. Construct each pair to differ minimally — ideally at exactly one faulty transition sharing an otherwise-correct prefix — so the contrast isolates the error instead of confounding it with unrelated variation.
4. Optimize the target against the manufactured signal with a contrastive/preference objective, score-conditioning, or distillation — not plain label-fitting. Where possible, reformulate the alignment objective inside the model's own native training loss rather than grafting a foreign objective across structurally different model families, which silently introduces an approximation gap. Changing only the granularity or only the label source of an off-the-shelf objective, with no such reformulation, is precisely what reviewers read as incremental.
5. Demonstrate that the gain is attributable to the preference structure itself, not merely to extra fine-tuning or incidental properties of the source. Ablate against self-improvement and same-compute baselines, and close the loop to the end-goal metric rather than reporting only reward-model accuracy. Bundle the move with a payoff a bare substitution cannot claim — a removed approximation, a capability previously assumed impossible, or a newly-surfaced failure axis.

## Differentiation within parent

Both this cluster and cluster 8 manufacture supervision in a self-referential loop, but they differ in the type of signal and the machinery that consumes it. Cluster 8 assigns provisional point labels to unlabeled outputs, applies an adaptive confidence cutoff to filter which labels to retain, and retrains a producer/evaluator pair on the filtered set each round — its engineering lives in the filter and the iteration schedule. This cluster instead manufactures a relative preference or scalar reward (pairwise pairs, typed dense rewards, internal output scores, tree-search orderings) and optimizes the target with a contrastive/preference objective rather than label-fitting; its engineering lives in how the comparison is sourced and granularized. It also diverges sharply from cluster 7, whose tactic stabilizes optimizer dynamics, initialization, and premature convergence for low-data adaptation and involves no preference or reward signal at all.

## When to pick this one

Pick this when the missing supervision is naturally comparative — you can say output A is better than B, or assign each output a quality score — rather than a single correct label, and you have a contrastive/preference objective ready to consume that ordering. Choose it especially when credit assignment is the pain point, because a holistic end-of-output scalar cannot localize which step or quality dimension failed.

## Tactical failure mode

The dominant failure is the single-component swap: the paper takes the established preference pipeline (reward model → contrastive optimization) and changes exactly one slot — human labels to an AI judge, offline data to online sampling, response-level to step-level granularity — then presents that swap as the contribution. Reviewers read this as incremental because the swap is a predictable synthesis of two already-known ideas and unlocks no behavior that was previously unreachable. A second shape is stopping short of the loop: shipping a preference dataset, benchmark, or reward model without demonstrating the downstream optimization that is supposed to consume it, so the work reads as infrastructure rather than method — or validating only on a proxy/surrogate domain without showing transfer to the target end-goal metric. A third is assembling off-the-shelf parts (parameter-efficient adaptation plus self-distillation) for a known problem while concurrent work does the same, leaving no defensible novelty. A candidate author should self-check: does the manufactured-preference move come bundled with a reformulation that removes an approximation, a demonstrated capability previously assumed impossible, or a newly-surfaced failure axis — or is it just relabeling one box in the standard diagram?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Embedding the preference signal into the primary training objective beats post-hoc correction, because behaviors entrenched during primary training cost more to unlearn than to never learn — alignment at training time achieves what a later correction stage structurally cannot.
- Reformulating preference alignment inside the model's own native pretraining loss eliminates the approximation gap that appears when an objective designed for one model family is ported to a structurally different one.
- A model's own internal output scores already encode a latent comparative ordering, so surfacing the signal inward dissolves the assumed dependence on an external annotation oracle.
- When absolute quality estimates are too noisy for pointwise regression, converting them into pairwise relative orderings recovers a learnable signal from the very same noisy source.
- Preference optimization over a diverse suite of problems can instill a transferable meta-skill rather than memorized per-instance solutions, enabling zero-shot transfer to unseen instances.
- Constructing preference pairs that explicitly span every distinct failure mode turns single-axis alignment into simultaneous multi-error suppression.

### Reject lessons
- Swapping the preference-label source (human → AI judge, offline → online) without showing the swap unlocks a behavior previously unreachable reads as a one-component substitution and draws novelty rejections.
- Stopping at the preference dataset, benchmark, or reward model without demonstrating the downstream optimization it is meant to feed leaves the contribution looking like infrastructure rather than method.
- Changing only the granularity of an established contrastive objective (response-level → step-level) is judged incremental unless paired with a clearly demonstrated practical unlock.
- Borrowing a structurally related proxy domain to synthesize preference data, without validating that the proxy's regularities transfer to the target's end-goal metric, invites rigor and generalization concerns.
- Combining off-the-shelf components (parameter-efficient adaptation plus self-distillation) for a known problem fails the novelty bar when concurrent work performs the same combination.
