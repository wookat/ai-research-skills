# C23
_parent: **Liberate a Fixed Generative Component** (`generative_process_redesign`)_

## Tactical pattern

Reach inside an iterative refinement (diffusion/flow) chain and re-specify one of its conventionally-frozen internal components, then re-derive the chain around the new choice. The objects swapped are concrete: the terminal prior/endpoint (replace isotropic noise with a structured task-adjacent reference bridged by incremental residual transfer), the per-step distribution family (replace the unimodal Gaussian with an adversarial or otherwise expressive multimodal conditional), the noise/solver discretization (choose step sizes and schedule to maximize a likelihood bound), the initial seed (backprop through the frozen chain to optimize it as the sole control variable), the forward noising process (a structure-aware random walk whose convergence theory sets the schedule), or the point along the schedule where the conditioning signal is injected. The swap is licensed by a structural property of the task — small target-reference residual, sequential correlation of outputs, algebraic/geometric structure of the output space, or the seed deterministically fixing the output — rather than by mere "we can vary it." The payoff is almost always collapsing hundreds of steps into a handful, or retargeting a pretrained procedure toward control, distributional sampling, or posterior inference without retraining its backbone.

## Step-by-Step

1. Locate a component of the iterative chain that everyone inherits as a fixed default — the terminal prior, the per-step distribution family, the noise/solver schedule, the initial seed, the forward noising kernel, or the point where conditioning is injected — and state precisely why it was fixed (a tractability convenience or an infinitesimal-step-limit assumption, not an actual task constraint). Naming the original justification is what lets you argue it is removable.
2. Identify a structural property of YOUR task that makes the default wasteful or suboptimal: the reference output is informationally adjacent to the target, consecutive outputs correlate, the output space carries algebraic or geometric structure, or the seed deterministically fixes the output. The redesign must be licensed by this property — a swap motivated only by 'this variable can be changed,' with no task-structural reason, reads as arbitrary recombination and is the first thing reviewers discount.
3. Re-derive the chain around the new component choice and produce the load-bearing object — a residual-transfer transition operator, an expressive reverse family, a likelihood-maximizing solver, an inductive distributional-consistency constraint — together with an argument that the redesigned chain still targets the correct distribution. A local heuristic substitution with no derivation showing it does something the prior object provably could not is the most common rejection shape.
4. Benchmark the gain against the STRONGEST existing method that already attacks the same bottleneck — few-step distillation, consistency models, fast solvers — not merely against the un-accelerated chain. Beating only the naive baseline, or omitting the dominant accelerator entirely, is fatal even when the redesign is sound, because the move then looks dominated rather than enabling.
5. Account for every extra artifact or assumption your redesign smuggles in — a separately trained auxiliary network, access to a simulator or dynamics model, added training cost — and show the net gain survives it. A hidden dependency that the claimed efficiency silently relies on collapses the contribution once a reviewer surfaces it, so audit your own pipeline for it before submission.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster reaches INSIDE the refinement mechanism and treats its internal degrees of freedom — prior/endpoint, per-step distribution, schedule/solver, seed, forward kernel, conditioning checkpoint — as the free design variables, almost always to chase inference efficiency or training-free retargeting while the output domain stays fixed. That is distinct from cluster 24, which ports the refinement mechanism wholesale to a NEW output domain (direct high-resolution sequential synthesis): there the chain is a transplantable black box adapted to a new data type, whereas here the data type is held constant and the chain's guts are the object of redesign. It is also distinct from cluster 15, which repurposes a pretrained generative model as an external implicit-supervision source for recovering hidden structure in a downstream inference task; cluster 15 uses the model as a tool, while cluster 23 re-engineers the generative procedure's own internal mechanics.

## When to pick this one

Pick this cluster when the bottleneck is the cost or rigidity of the refinement chain itself — too many sampling steps, an uninformative starting point that wastes most of the trajectory, or a need for training-free control/conditioning over a frozen backbone — and you can point to a structural property of your task that lets you re-specify one internal component (prior, schedule, seed, per-step distribution, or conditioning point) rather than retrain or re-domain the procedure.

## Tactical failure mode

The recurring failure is treating "I re-specified component X" as self-evident novelty: a single local swap is made (a context-derived starting distribution, one x0-approximation traded for another, a seed reparameterization) that is locally reasonable but neither re-derives the chain nor demonstrates a regime the dominant accelerated alternative cannot already reach. These attempts consistently fail to beat — or even compare against — the strongest existing fast-sampling method (consistency models, distillation, fast solvers), so the contribution reads as dominated rather than enabling. A second shape is the hidden artifact: the claimed efficiency silently leans on an extra resource (access to a dynamics model or simulator, a separately trained auxiliary network) whose cost cancels the advertised gain. A third is the absent load-bearing justification — no theoretical statement, no failure-regime analysis — leaving the redesign indistinguishable from recombining known parts. Self-check: if your only claim is that component X was previously fixed and you varied it, and you cannot name a concrete regime where the dominant accelerator fails but your redesign succeeds, you are in this failure mode.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- The terminal prior of a refinement chain is a free design variable — anchoring it at a structured, task-adjacent reference rather than pure noise geometrically shortens the trajectory and cuts the step count by an order of magnitude.
- The per-step unimodal distribution is only valid in the infinitesimal-step limit; replacing it with an expressive multimodal conditional lets the chain take large steps and reach few-step inference without sacrificing coverage.
- Because the initial seed deterministically determines the output under a frozen procedure, optimizing the seed alone via backprop through the whole chain yields fine-grained, training-free control across heterogeneous objectives.
- A pretrained deterministic regressor already meets the structural requirements of an interpolation-based sampler, so fine-tuning it inside a refinement loop converts a point-estimator into a distributional generator without architectural redesign.
- Borrowing convergence theory from a well-characterized stochastic process to set the forward noising kernel and step count turns schedule design from tuning into a derived quantity.
- Where the conditioning signal is evaluated along the schedule is itself a tunable variable — relocating it to a lower-perturbation checkpoint cuts the conditioning-approximation error with no retraining of the base procedure.

### Reject lessons
- Re-specifying the chain's starting distribution is sound but worthless if a dominant accelerator already reaches one or two steps — failing to beat the strongest fast-sampling alternative dooms the move regardless of its elegance.
- Substituting one in-support approximation for another without theoretical depth or a failure-regime analysis reads as a narrow heuristic swap rather than a contribution.
- A redesign that silently depends on an extra artifact — access to a simulator/dynamics model, or a separately trained auxiliary network — has its claimed efficiency cancelled by the hidden cost once a reviewer surfaces it.
- Collapsing a staged procedure into a single differentiable mapping is technically clean but gets rejected when the novelty is thin against recent methods and the gains are demonstrated only on weak or dated benchmarks.
