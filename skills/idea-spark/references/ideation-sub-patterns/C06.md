# C06
_parent: **Prove Equivalence to Unify** (`algebraic_equivalence_unification`)_

## Tactical pattern

This cluster operates specifically on probabilistic training objectives — divergences, likelihoods, surrogate losses, variational bounds, and distributional supervision signals — rather than on generic procedures. The signature move is to take an objective currently treated as a fixed default, an unexplained heuristic, or a quantity estimated across multiple stages, prove it is algebraically a special case of (or exactly equal to) a broader parametric divergence family or a principled probabilistic quantity — an ELBO, a log-density gradient, a log-partition function, a decision-theoretic uncertainty functional — and then cash that identity three recurring ways: collapse a multi-stage estimation pipeline into a single loss by showing the intermediate quantity is encoded in the final objective, substitute a better-conditioned member of the now-visible family, or solve in closed form for the optimal member. The objects are concrete — KL versus total-variation versus Rényi versus α-β divergence, reverse-process variance, reward-model logits read as a function of the policy ratio, sample-similarity versus feature-correlation matrices under double normalization — and the verbs are reparameterize, re-anchor, integrate-over-scale, and transpose. The unification is always made load-bearing: the equivalence eliminates a learned module, removes a numerical instability, or yields a provable rate, never merely relabeling.

## Step-by-Step

1. Take an objective the field treats as the single 'natural' choice, an unexplained heuristic, or a multi-stage estimation pipeline, and pin down the implicit distributional quantity it acts on — the divergence being minimized, the likelihood or bound being maximized, the supervision signal being matched. Name the tacit assumption that froze it in that form: that one divergence is canonical, that the intermediate quantity must be learned separately, or that hard/one-hot targets are necessary.
2. Prove an exact algebraic identity that places the objective inside a larger structure — show it is one member of a parametric divergence family, or that it equals a principled probabilistic quantity under a precisely stated constraint (a normalization condition, a residual distribution, a monotone weighting). Keep the identity tight and load-bearing: if the connection is only a loose analogy, or a reinterpretation that merely adds a free parameter without changing what is computed, the move degrades into a hyperparameter study.
3. Cash the identity for concrete leverage instead of relabeling. Collapse the pipeline by showing the intermediate quantity is algebraically encoded in the final objective; or swap in the family member that fixes a NAMED pathology of the original (mass leakage, gradient asymmetry, instability at a regularization limit); or derive the optimal member in closed form. The exploit must change what is computed — remove a learned component, eliminate an instability, or unlock a rate.
4. Make the equivalence's preconditions auditable. State the normalization constraints, regularity conditions, or distributional assumptions the identity requires, and check them empirically rather than asserting them. Equivalence claims that rest on a precondition which may not hold for actually-trained models, or on a vague 'sufficient sample size,' leave the identity hollow precisely where it is invoked.
5. Position the result so it both legitimizes and surpasses the status quo: demonstrate that known methods reappear as special cases (this makes the unification credible) and instantiate at least one genuinely new member that beats them on their own metric while delivering the structural benefit. If special cases only recover prior work with marginal gains and no new member earns its keep, the contribution reads as incremental and the unification appears decorative.

## When to pick this one

Pick this instantiation when the distinct procedures you suspect coincide are themselves probabilistic objectives — divergences, likelihoods, surrogate losses, or distributional supervision signals — and the payoff is collapsing an estimation pipeline, substituting a better-conditioned divergence, or solving analytically for the optimal family member. If the objects are architectural modules, combinatorial algorithms, or non-probabilistic procedures, the equivalence belongs to a different region of the parent.

## Tactical failure mode

The recurring failure is the cosmetic generalization: the paper embeds an existing objective in a one-parameter divergence family (or relabels an existing method as a special case of its framework) but never shows the new degree of freedom repairs a concrete, named failure of the original. The wrong assumption is that more generality is itself a contribution, so the work cannot point to a specific pathology of the baseline objective that its member provably and uniquely fixes, and the added scalar reads as one more hyperparameter to tune. A second shape is the unverified equivalence — an unbiasedness, optimality, or contraction claim resting on a precondition (a normalization, a residual distribution, optimality of a trained model, a 'sufficient' upstream sample size) that is asserted rather than checked, leaving the identity empty exactly where it is needed. Such papers typically show only marginal gains over the method they generalize, omit the direct baseline comparison that would isolate the family's benefit, or overlap with a previously published loss. A candidate author should self-check: does my equivalence eliminate a module, kill an instability, or earn a rate — and does a genuinely new member beat the old one on its own metric?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Reinterpreting a model's pre-normalization scalar outputs as unnormalized log-densities lets you re-derive an existing signal as a generative quantity, unlocking a hypothesis that could not even be stated before the identity.
- Proving that an optimal per-stage parameter has a closed form in terms of an already-learned signal eliminates a whole learned or hand-tuned component and works training-free on any pretrained model.
- Showing two rival objective families are the same matrix-norm penalty viewed along transposed axes dissolves an apparent design schism and lets each family inherit the other's beneficial properties.
- A change of variables expressing a latent intermediate quantity as a function of the final-to-reference solution ratio makes the intermediate estimation stage algebraically redundant, collapsing a multi-stage pipeline into a single classification loss.
- Recasting a family of empirically-tuned losses as a monotonically-weighted integral of a principled lower bound converts heuristic criterion selection into a principled design space — choosing an augmentation distribution instead of departing from theory.
- Importing a closed-form optimality result from a neighboring field (extreme-value statistics, statistical mechanics, decision theory) can supply the exact analytic mechanism a long-standing instability has been missing.

### Reject lessons
- Generalizing a method to a one-parameter divergence family without demonstrating that the new freedom fixes the method's actual named shortcoming reads as an empirical hyperparameter study, not a contribution.
- Swapping a standard divergence for a fancier one to 'reduce bias' fails when you cannot establish a concrete failure mode of the original that the replacement provably and uniquely repairs.
- Reinterpreting an existing loss as a special case of your framework while delivering only marginal gains over that loss leaves reviewers unable to isolate what the framework actually buys.
- A theoretically-motivated bias correction that does not translate into empirical gains over strong (even if biased) baselines, and rests on an unverifiable sufficiency condition, is judged incremental on both the theoretical and the practical axis.
- Soft-target or divergence reformulations that overlap with a prior published loss and show only marginal improvement on already-strong models suggest the targeted problem was implicitly solved, leaving no isolable contribution.
