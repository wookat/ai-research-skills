# C02
_parent: **Design a Confound-Isolating Diagnostic** (`controlled_diagnostic_design`)_

## Tactical pattern

The shared move is to construct a controlled set of evaluation instances in which exactly one named confound is either structurally blocked or independently varied, then report the divergence between apparent and de-confounded performance as the primary result rather than an aggregate leaderboard score. Concretely the authors do one of: parameterize instances as templates with typed slots and consistency constraints so surface form varies independently of reasoning structure; generate instances from a formal specification so intermediate steps are verifiable; gate instances by provenance timestamp relative to each system's training cutoff; stratify instances by realism (pedagogical versus production); suppress one input cue or withhold one modality at a time; randomize answer-option order; or build same-source positive/negative pairs with controlled defect injection. The confounds attacked are recurring and specific — memorization/contamination, surface style, the generating source's capability, single-modality shortcuts, scoring-function nonlinearity, and task-format exposure — and the load-bearing artifact is always the controlled instance set paired with an oracle independent of the system under test. The headline finding is the measured gap itself (a transition that vanishes under a linear metric, a score that collapses when an irrelevant clause is inserted, an 87%-versus-37% split between pedagogical and production partitions), used to overturn a face-value reading of an existing measurement.

## Step-by-Step

1. Name the exact confound and the true property it masks. Write down the single manipulable artifact you believe is inflating the current measurement — a memorized test set, a surface-style cue, the generating source's capability, a single-modality shortcut, a nonlinear scoring function, or task-format exposure — and state precisely which property it is being mistaken for. A diffuse 'this benchmark is flawed' framing is insufficient; instruments whose target confound is never pinned to one variable are turned away as vague.
2. Construct controlled instances that block or independently vary that one confound while holding everything else fixed. Use synthetic generation from a formal spec, typed-slot templates with consistency constraints, per-cue suppression, provenance/timestamp gating, realism stratification, or matched same-source pairs with injected defects — whatever isolates the chosen axis. Keep all other axes constant so any measured difference is attributable to the confound alone; uncontrolled co-variation between surface change and structural change is the most common reason the isolation claim fails.
3. Supply ground truth that is independent of the system under evaluation. Verifiability must come from how the instance was constructed — a known derivation path, a planted failure region, a mechanistic pass/fail criterion, an exchangeability-based null distribution — never from the evaluand's own consensus or a self-asserted, contested definition. Designs whose oracle is the very population being judged, or that hardcode the definition they claim to test, are rejected for circularity.
4. Report the divergence between apparent and de-confounded performance as the headline result, and ensure it reveals something not knowable in advance. The contribution is the gap itself — a discontinuity that disappears, a score that drops sharply, a partition that splits cleanly — not a restatement of a limitation the field already accepted. An instrument that merely re-confirms a documented shortcut, surfacing no previously unmeasurable failure, reads as confirmatory and is declined.
5. Bound the claim to exactly what the instances probe and demonstrate the isolated signal survives outside the controlled regime. Show transfer from synthetic to realistic conditions, or explicitly scope the conclusion to the task structure actually tested. A narrow single-task or small-N instrument carrying broad capability claims invites a scope-mismatch rejection, and a synthetic-only result with no real-world corroboration invites an ecological-validity rejection.

## When to pick this one

Reach for this when you can name a specific shortcut or artifact you suspect is inflating a reported measurement AND you can construct instances that hold it fixed or vary it independently while an oracle independent of the system under test scores the de-confounded instances. If you cannot block the confound without retreating to an artificial regime, or your only available ground truth is the evaluand itself, this tactic will collapse into circularity.

## Tactical failure mode

Papers attempting this tactic fail most often when the constructed instrument isolates the confound only inside an artificial regime and never demonstrates that the isolated signal transfers to real deployment conditions — the synthetic-to-real gap is asserted rather than shown. A second recurring shape is circularity: the diagnostic is validated against the same definition, assumption, or system population it was built to interrogate (using the evaluated models as their own oracle, or hardcoding a contested definition of the target property), so it cannot independently confirm what it claims. A third is the confirmatory benchmark — the instrument re-derives an already-documented limitation and surfaces nothing that was not knowable in advance, leaving reviewers without an actionable new finding. Finally, scope-claim mismatch (a single-task or small-N instrument carrying broad capability claims) and pure engineering assembly (stacking retrieval/knowledge-base/pipeline blocks with no isolating insight) both read as insufficient contribution. A candidate can self-check against three questions: does the instrument surface a genuinely new, previously-unmeasurable gap; is its ground truth independent of the evaluand; and has the isolated signal been shown to hold outside the controlled regime.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Treat the scoring function as an active independent variable: swapping a nonlinear metric for a continuous one on the same fixed outputs can dissolve an apparent capability discontinuity, proving it was a measurement artifact rather than a real jump.
- When systems differ in their exposure to a confound, equalize by applying the confounding treatment uniformly to all of them rather than trying to strip it out of the advantaged ones.
- A forced-choice contest between two cues measures relative salience, not load-bearing reliance; suppressing each candidate cue independently and measuring the resulting performance drop yields a confound-free dependence score.
- Sourcing evaluation instances with provenance timestamps later than each system's training cutoff converts contamination from an unknowable confound into a structurally eliminable, empirically observable phenomenon.
- Inverting the difficulty axis — choosing tasks trivial for the reference population but requiring reliable multi-step orchestration — exposes a capability gap that difficulty-scaled instruments are structurally unable to surface.
- Measuring coverage at large sample counts instead of single-sample accuracy distinguishes whether an optimization procedure expands the solvable set or merely resharpens probability mass over capabilities the base system already had.

### Reject lessons
- A diagnostic that only re-confirms a shortcut already known in advance, surfacing no previously unmeasurable failure, is read as confirmatory rather than as a contribution.
- Validating a synthetic-ground-truth instrument only on synthetic data, without demonstrating that the controlled finding transfers to real-world instances, leaves ecological validity unestablished and undermines the central claim.
- Building the instrument around one contested definition of the target property means it cannot independently confirm that the definition is correct, and reviewers flag the resulting circularity.
- Resting an unsupervised evaluation on the unverified premise that the systems under test can reliably judge one another stakes the entire method on an assumption that collapses under scrutiny.
- Pairing a single-task or small-sample instrument with broad capability claims triggers a scope-mismatch rejection, and assembling retrieval or engineering blocks without an isolating scientific insight reads as application rather than research.
