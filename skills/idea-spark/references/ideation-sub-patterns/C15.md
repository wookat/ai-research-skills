# C15
_parent: **Liberate a Fixed Generative Component** (`generative_process_redesign`)_

## Tactical pattern

These papers re-choose the geometric/structural representation that a 3D synthesis or reconstruction procedure operates over, treating the conventionally-inherited parameterization as a tractability convenience rather than an actual requirement. Concrete moves recur: replace continuous implicit fields (NeRF/SDF MLPs) with explicit discrete primitives (3D Gaussians, sculpted neural points, sphere-initialized tetrahedra); re-parameterize variable-connectivity meshes as a fixed-resolution deformable tetrahedral grid so a standard fixed-tensor diffusion model applies; swap an unsigned distance function for a signed field on an enclosing watertight domain to make open surfaces extractable; store features in explicit sparse 3D voxel grids instead of factored tri-plane latents; or substitute generated pseudo-observations (multi-view, canonical-frame) for missing real ones so an unchanged downstream estimator becomes well-posed. The substitution itself is the load-bearing contribution — the surrounding generative, feed-forward, or simulation machinery is deliberately left standard, and the gain (order-of-magnitude speedup, higher fidelity, a previously unreachable output class) is attributed to the representation alone. The free variable being liberated is the spatial substrate the engine reads and writes, not the engine.

## Step-by-Step

1. Name the geometric/structural representation your pipeline inherited by default and ask what it was actually chosen for — almost always the tractability of an upstream step, not the task's true requirement. Pin down the specific downstream failure that representation forces (occupancy pruning that needs dense consistent multi-view signals, unsigned fields that break bisection-based contour extraction, variable connectivity that blocks fixed-tensor generators, factored latents that cap fidelity).
2. Select an alternative representation whose intrinsic structural property removes that failure by construction rather than by an added regularizer. The strongest substitutions exploit a property the alternative already has — a signed field's interior/exterior sign, a grid's identical tensor layout across instances, a volumetric primitive's internal connectivity, an implicit query's constant-time distance — instead of bolting a correction onto the old representation. A swap that merely carries an existing representation into a harder input regime without such a property reads as incremental.
3. Hold the rest of the pipeline fixed and route the swap so the heavy machinery (diffusion, feed-forward reconstructor, simulator, estimator) consumes or emits the new representation unchanged. The contribution should be the substitution; resist accreting bespoke encoders, tokens, or loss terms whose individual necessity you cannot defend, since they dilute the claim into 'engineering integration.'
4. Demonstrate the capability the old representation could not deliver — order-of-magnitude speed, fidelity on a previously unreachable output class, scale to far more elements — and isolate it with an ablation that swaps the representation back to confirm the gain is the representation's doing and not a co-varying trick.
5. Prove the old representation was a false constraint by exhibiting the concrete regime where it provably fails and the new one does not (sparse or uneven inputs, ambiguous supervision gradients, open or non-watertight boundaries). Showing only equivalence, or gains that are marginal and untraceable to the swap, is the dominant reason sound versions of this tactic get rejected.

Compactly: cluster 24 changes the engine's domain, cluster 23 changes what conditions the engine, and this cluster changes the geometric representation the engine reads and writes while leaving the engine intact.

## When to pick this one

Pick this when your pipeline's bottleneck — slow convergence, capped fidelity, an output class it cannot reach — traces to the geometric/structural representation it inherited (continuous implicit fields, variable-connectivity meshes, unsigned distances, factored latents, posed-geometry assumptions) rather than to the learning procedure or the loss. The decisive signal is that the representation was adopted for upstream convenience and the task never actually required it.

## Tactical failure mode

The reject papers do execute the representation swap, but fall short on proving necessity. The most common shape: the new representation is adopted to handle a harder input regime (sparse or unevenly sampled data, environment-specific signals, higher resolution) but the paper never exhibits a controlled setting where the prior representation provably fails and theirs does not — so reviewers read the swap as an incremental extension of an existing representation rather than the liberation of a false constraint. A second shape: empirical gains over strong baselines are marginal or not isolated to the representation change, leaving 'why is this representation uniquely necessary?' unanswered. A third: the core substitution is real but the paper accretes auxiliary modules — extra encoders, conditioning tokens, loss terms — whose individual necessity is unjustified, so the contribution dissolves into engineering integration with thin perceived novelty. A fourth: the paper claims a structural property the representation should confer (cross-view consistency, validity under symmetry, generalization) without demonstrating it. Self-check: can you point to the regime where the old representation causes measurable downstream harm, and an ablation that swaps back to confirm the gain is the representation's doing?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- A plain observational generative model's learned distribution already encodes enough multi-condition variation to act as implicit structural supervision — projecting a coarse guess onto its manifold turns an implausible sample into a usable pseudo-observation, no structural labels needed.
- Re-parameterizing a variable-structure object through a fixed-resolution deformable grid pushes structural variation into per-node scalar values, giving every instance an identical tensor layout so off-the-shelf fixed-input generators apply with zero specialized machinery.
- Treating an upstream-produced discrete scaffold as a mutable, differentiable object the loss can reshape (insert and prune elements) — rather than a fixed input to accept — lets explicit representations match implicit ones at a fraction of inference cost.
- A hard representational problem (open or non-watertight boundaries) collapses to a tractable one by embedding the object as a delimited region inside a simpler complete enclosing domain where standard signed-field extraction works.
- An ill-posed inverse problem becomes well-posed by generating the missing observations, or by predicting everything in one canonical reference frame — so the downstream estimator needs no change and a long-assumed input dependency (camera poses, dense capture) simply disappears.
- Output quality is an emergent property of inter-element interactions, so it must be baked into each primitive's internal organization (volumetric connectivity) rather than imposed as a post-hoc cleanup pass over isolated elements.

### Reject lessons
- Swapping a representation to survive a harder input regime, without exhibiting a controlled setting where the old representation provably fails, reads as an extension rather than a liberation.
- Sound theoretical correctness of a new representation does not survive review when empirical gains over strong baselines are marginal and not shown to fix a real downstream failure mode of competitors.
- A representation borrowed from a neighboring subfield and ported into a new domain is judged incremental unless the paper states exactly what the port resolves that prior representations could not.
- Stacking auxiliary encoders, conditioning tokens, and loss terms around the core substitution without justifying each component's necessity dissolves the contribution into engineering integration with thin novelty.
- Claiming a structural property the chosen representation should confer — cross-view consistency, validity under symmetry, generalization — without demonstrating it leaves reviewers unconvinced the representation actually delivers the advertised benefit.
