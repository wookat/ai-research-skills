# C05
_parent: **Audit and Pivot an Assumption** (`assumption_audit_and_pivot`)_

## Tactical pattern

The shared move is to take an existing identifiability or recovery theorem for hidden structure and pivot on the assumption it requires for that structure to be uniquely recoverable. The dominant swap is trading a distributional assumption — statistical independence of latent factors, a specific parametric/Gaussian or linear mixing map, or access to labels and controlled perturbations — for a structural, architectural, or geometric one: additive or block-separable decoders, an exclusive-assignment support pattern in the generator's Jacobian, feasible-region or inverse-curvature signatures that survive arbitrary smooth mixing, or sparsity imposed on temporal increments and inter-factor influence across ordered windows. A second variant relaxes an all-or-nothing structural regularity to partial/local compliance, or lifts a recovery rule from IID to exchangeable/non-IID data by substituting the correct latent-variable decomposition for the independence factorization. In every case the payoff is a re-proven recovery of the hidden factors, causal graph, or model parameters up to the field's standard ambiguity class (permutation, sign-flip, component-wise invertible reparameterization, or linear transform), then operationalized as a regularizer or estimator.

## Step-by-Step

1. Pin down the single assumption the existing recovery or identifiability result rests on — statistical independence of the hidden factors, a linear or specific parametric mixing/observation map, access to labels or controlled perturbations, IID sampling, or an all-or-nothing structural regularity every component must satisfy. State it so precisely that the prior proof demonstrably collapses without it. If you cannot show the proof breaks when the assumption is removed, the assumption is not load-bearing and the pivot will read as cosmetic.
2. Identify a substitute condition of a DIFFERENT kind that carries the same identifying power but that your setting plausibly satisfies. The highest-yield swaps trade a distributional assumption for a structural, architectural, or geometric one — additive or block separability of the decoder, an exclusive-assignment support pattern, a feasible-region or curvature signature that survives nonlinear transformation, sparsity over temporal increments — or trade global compliance for local/partial compliance. Avoid merely bolting one framework onto another while keeping both original assumptions; a union of two methods rarely earns a new guarantee.
3. Re-derive recovery up to the field's accepted ambiguity class — permutation, sign-flip, component-wise invertible reparameterization, or linear transform — and prove your substitute condition ALONE is sufficient, not merely correlated with recovery. Name the equivalence class explicitly and exhibit it; a guarantee that cannot state what it recovers things up to is not yet a guarantee, and asserting 'consistency' or 'identifiability' the proof never establishes collapses the contribution under review.
4. Confirm the pivot opens a genuinely new regime rather than relabeling the old one: exhibit a concrete setting your relaxed condition admits and the prior assumption excluded, and verify the new condition is checkable or enforceable in practice. If the relaxed condition is as hard to verify as the original was to satisfy, or if it silently smuggles the old assumption back in, the contribution will not survive scrutiny.
5. Operationalize the theorem as a regularizer, estimator, or algorithm that actually enforces the condition the proof requires, and demonstrate recovery empirically precisely where the original assumption fails. Close the theory-practice gap — an identifiability condition the algorithm neither enforces nor verifies undermines both soundness and significance — and show the ambiguity class being resolved on data the prior method could not handle.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster's pivot targets an IDENTIFIABILITY assumption and re-proves recovery of hidden latent, causal, or generative structure up to an ambiguity class — the deliverable is a recovery theorem with a named equivalence class. Sibling cluster 1 also lives in representation learning but its tactic is architectural mechanism design (instantiating a structural prior as a dual-branch distributional-divergence module); it adds a mechanism, not a proof that the latent structure is recoverable. Sibling cluster 29 likewise 'establishes a new property,' yet its object is a projection operator's descent-implies-distance property in constrained optimization, yielding stability classifications of stationary points — a guarantee about optimization iterates, not about recovering ground-truth latent factors. Clusters 11 and 19 also pivot assumptions, but to tighten privacy-composition and distribution-free generalization bounds respectively, again not to identify hidden structure. The signature of cluster 5 versus all of these is the phrase 'recoverable up to permutation/sign/linear transform' as the proof's terminal claim.

## When to pick this one

Pick this when your goal is to recover hidden/latent/causal/generative structure and the bottleneck is an existing identifiability result whose precondition fails in your setting. The decisive signal is that a different KIND of constraint already present in your data or architecture (a structural separability, a geometric signature, sparsity, sequential context) plausibly carries the identifying information the prior distributional or parametric assumption was supplying.

## Tactical failure mode

The signature failure is an identifiability claim that outruns its proof. Papers fail when the pivoted assumption was not actually load-bearing — the new setting is a straightforward union of two existing frameworks (a separation method plus a constrained or multi-view wrapper), so re-proving recovery for the union reads as incremental rather than as opening a new regime. A second shape is asserting recovery or 'consistency' while the decisive object is never established: latent identifiability left unproven, part of the model excluded from the guarantee, or a non-convex objective claimed solved without strong duality. A third is imposing structural constraints to motivate interpretability or disentanglement without proving they are SUFFICIENT for recovery, leaving the result indistinguishable from a post-hoc heuristic — sometimes compounded by formal errors in the conditional-independence bookkeeping that void the central theorem even when experiments look reasonable. Self-check: can you exhibit the exact equivalence class you recover, prove your substitute condition alone suffices (not merely that the prior method also worked), and show your algorithm enforces the very condition your theorem requires? If any of these is missing, the pivot has not yet been earned.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- A purely architectural constraint on the generative mapping — additive or block separability of the decoder — can fully substitute for distributional independence of the latents, shifting the identification burden from the prior to the geometry of the function.
- Geometric signatures such as feasible-region independence or low-rank inverse-curvature structure survive arbitrary smooth nonlinear mixing where algebraic or parametric structure does not, unlocking identifiability without any linearity assumption.
- Relaxing an all-or-nothing structural condition to 'partial compliance recovers the compliant subset' converts a brittle universal guarantee into a usable one without weakening what it claims about the components that do comply.
- Sparsity over temporal increments or over an inter-factor influence structure across ordered windows can replace auxiliary labels and controlled perturbations as the symmetry-breaking signal, yielding identifiability from purely observational data.
- Showing that a standard supervised or discriminative objective implicitly instantiates the conditioning structure of an identifiability theory imports recovery guarantees into settings believed to lie outside that theory's reach.
- Extending a recovery rule from IID to exchangeable/non-IID data by swapping the independence factorization for the correct latent-variable decomposition restores tractable identification conditions where the standard rule silently fails.

### Reject lessons
- Stacking two established frameworks and re-proving identifiability of the union reads as incremental unless the pivoted assumption was genuinely load-bearing and the union admits a regime neither framework could reach alone.
- Advertising an identifiability or 'consistency' property while the proof omits the decisive object — never proving latent identifiability, or excluding part of the model from the guarantee — collapses the contribution the moment a reviewer checks the theorem.
- Imposing structural constraints to claim interpretability or disentanglement without proving those constraints are sufficient for recovery leaves the result indistinguishable from a post-hoc heuristic.
- Proving identifiability conditions the algorithm neither enforces nor verifies creates a theory-practice disconnect that undermines both the soundness and the significance of the claim.
- Errors in the dependency or conditional-independence bookkeeping (mislabeling an independence, opening a collider) void the central theorem even when the empirical numbers look reasonable.
