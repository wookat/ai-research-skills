# C10
_parent: **Characterize a Limit, Then Surpass It** (`characterize_limit_then_surpass`)_

## Tactical pattern

Take an iterative, locally-scoped procedure over a relational or combinatorial structure — neighborhood message-passing, higher-order message passing on complexes, persistence filtration, or per-pivot/per-anchor enumeration — and pinpoint the one structural object it discards for tractability: the overlap pattern among co-occurring neighbors, global geodesic/resistance distance, discrete curvature on edges, bounded-length paths between co-adjacent nodes, a substructure vocabulary, or a color/connectivity-separating set. Formalize the equivalence class this discarding induces as a WL-style separation (or indistinguishability) criterion stated on the procedure's native objects rather than on proxy encodings, then prove the established class conflates objects that differ only in the discarded property. Re-inject that exact object into the procedure's core step — the aggregation kernel, the refinement update, or the enumeration granularity — and prove the augmented operator strictly exceeds the criterion (or, stronger, is incomparable to the canonical hierarchy). The load-bearing constraint is bounding the added cost to linear, quadratic, or polynomial-delay, so the expressivity/efficiency gain avoids the superquadratic lift to higher-order joint representations that the field tacitly treats as the only route upward.

## Step-by-Step

1. Locate the tractability shortcut in an established iterative/local procedure and name the exact structural object it throws away — the unordered-multiset abstraction of local context, the absence of global pairwise distance, flat symmetric aggregation, or per-pivot recomputation. Boundary: this object must be something the procedure provably cannot recover, not merely something it handles suboptimally; a gap that is trivially implied by the model's own assumptions is too weak to count as a blindspot.
2. Formalize the blindspot as a precise separation or indistinguishability criterion on the procedure's NATIVE target objects, not on proxy representations of them. Build the combinatorial certificate — an equivalence class, an explicit pair of objects the procedure conflates, or a complexity barrier — that makes the limit checkable. Boundary: an informal analogy (e.g. 'both steps smooth high frequencies') is not a separation criterion and will be read as an underspecified mechanism.
3. Prove the established method class fails the criterion AND map the current frontier. Exhibit concrete objects the method conflates to show the limit is real, then verify that no already-published method strictly surpasses it. Boundary: a 'surpass' that a recent stronger method already subsumes, or that beats only an un-augmented strawman baseline, is redundant rather than novel.
4. Construct the augmented operator by re-injecting the discarded object into the procedure's core computational step so it becomes first-class structure rather than an attribute or a preprocessing artifact, and prove the result strictly exceeds the criterion (ideally proving incomparability to the canonical hierarchy, which stakes distinct territory). Boundary: a bolt-on formalism that does not demonstrably capture the specific missed invariant is abstraction without advantage.
5. Bound the added cost and benchmark against the strongest competitors. Prove the augmentation preserves tractability — linear/quadratic overhead or polynomial delay — and compare against the strongest expressivity/complexity methods, not just the base procedure. Boundary: unvalidated tractability claims, missing state-of-the-art baselines, or only modest measured gains will sink the contribution even when the theory is clean.

## When to pick this one

Instantiate as this cluster when the plateaued method is an iterative or locally-scoped procedure over a relational/combinatorial object (graph, cell complex, causal DAG, persistence diagram) and you can name a single discarded structural object — a distance, a cycle, a substructure, a separating set — that you can re-inject into the procedure's core step at bounded cost. As the sole instantiation under this parent, the decisive signal is simply this match of "iterative-local procedure + identifiable discarded structural primitive + re-injection point," rather than a contrast against an alternative tactic.

## Tactical failure mode

Papers attempting this tactic fail when the "limit" they characterize is not a genuine structural blindspot: it is either trivially implied by the model's own assumptions, already surpassed by a published method, or never formalized at all beyond an informal analogy. A recurring shape is substituting a hand-wavy mechanism ("both operations act as low-pass filters") for a separation proof, leaving reviewers unable to see why the augmented operator must exceed the baseline. Another shape is constructing the operator first and asserting superiority without surveying the frontier, so a recently published stronger method strictly subsumes the claimed gain — or, for pure-efficiency variants, existing implementations directly refute the novelty claim. A third shape ports a formalism to a new domain but never demonstrates it captures an invariant the incumbent provably misses, producing abstraction with no testable advantage and no baseline comparison. The self-check: can you exhibit concrete objects the established method conflates, prove that no already-published method separates them, and bound your added cost — if any one of the three is absent, the surpass claim is unsupported.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Inserting a computationally cheap intermediate granularity — strictly finer than the baseline's coarse equivalence but far cheaper than full structural matching — captures most of the discriminative signal at linear overhead.
- Importing a quantity from continuous geometry to serve as a directly computable certificate can convert a heuristically-described failure mode into a provable structural cause with a surgical remedy.
- Reframing the question from 'how do we ascend the operator-complexity hierarchy' into 'what specific structural information is structurally absent from local aggregation' often surfaces a cheaper, more targeted augmentation.
- A single combinatorial bridge that bijects a method's output descriptors to the structural features they encode can unlock the tight impossibility result and the recipe for surpassing it in one move.
- Proving your augmentation is incomparable to the canonical hierarchy — capturing patterns it provably cannot, not merely being 'stronger' — stakes out genuinely new territory that a subsumption result cannot.
- Porting a separation argument from proxy-object analysis onto a field's native target objects can reveal that the dominant framework provably cannot achieve the field's own stated purpose.

### Reject lessons
- Resting the central claim on an informal analogy (two operators 'both smooth high frequencies') instead of a separation proof leaves the mechanism underspecified and the empirical gain unconvincing.
- Applying existing structural descriptors to a new application domain without formalizing a new limit or constructing a new operator yields a domain study, not a methodological contribution, and reviewers find no transferable novelty.
- Claiming a 'maximally expressive' construction that a recently published method already strictly subsumes turns the surpass-the-limit claim into redundant work.
- A novel formalism that cannot generate predictions or demonstrate concrete advantages over existing definitions reads as abstraction for its own sake.
- Asserting the 'first efficient implementation' of a long-known algorithm without surveying the landscape invites direct refutation by existing faster implementations and collapses the novelty claim.
- Deriving a 'limitation' that is trivially implied by the model's own linearity assumption produces a limit too weak to count as a real blindspot, and the proposed surpass step stays under-validated.
