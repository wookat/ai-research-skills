# C30
_parent: **Substitute the Operator or Representation** (`architectural_operator_substitution`)_

## Tactical pattern

This cluster treats the second-order/curvature object of the *training dynamics* — the Hessian, Gauss-Newton matrix, limiting NTK/linearized kernel, Fisher/natural-gradient metric, or gradient-flow vector field — as the operator to substitute or reshape, then discharges a spectral proof obligation. Two concrete instantiations recur. (a) Replace exact curvature with a structurally cheaper surrogate that provably retains the load-bearing spectral content: a Kronecker-factored/block-diagonal approximation, a low-rank truncation, a fixed curvature proxy folded into a first-order step, or an exact equivalence that reframes a second-order ranking criterion as a first-order gradient-outlier statistic. (b) Reparameterize or lift the variable — degree-lifting, product-form factorization, value-preserving symmetry teleportation, early-detected low-dimensional update subspace — so the landscape's curvature spectrum is reshaped: spurious minima acquire negative curvature, or the limiting kernel stays strictly positive definite at a smaller rank. The load-bearing artifact is always a spectral guarantee — positive-definiteness of the composite/limiting curvature matrix, a saddle-escape or eigenvalue-threshold result, a conditioning-decoupled convergence rate, or a completeness theorem for conserved quantities.

## Step-by-Step

1. Name the exact second-order quantity that governs the outcome you care about — not 'curvature is expensive' but the specific spectral object: the smallest eigenvalue that throttles the step size, the outlier eigenvalues that create spurious minima, the limiting kernel's positive-definiteness, or the dimension of the conserved-quantity space. Tie the bottleneck (slow convergence, trapped optimization, infeasible attribution) to this one quantity. Attempts that merely observe 'second-order methods are costly' and reach for a generic proxy without isolating which spectral quantity is load-bearing will read as incremental.
2. Pick the substitution direction. Either (a) replace the object with a structurally cheaper surrogate that keeps the load-bearing spectral content, or (b) reparameterize/lift the variable so the spectrum itself is reshaped in your favor. The surrogate or reparameterization must exploit a structural property latent in *this* problem — low-rank update increments, a factorized parameterization, composability across operator types, an invariant subspace — rather than importing an off-the-shelf approximation that happens to be cheap.
3. Prove that the substitute preserves the essential spectral property for your actual structure. Establish positive-definiteness of the *composite* matrix (e.g. when operator-constrained terms mix direct and transformed evaluations), derive the saddle-escape or rank-threshold result, or show the cheap and expensive criteria are *exactly* equivalent. Do not assume a positive-definiteness or no-spurious-minima result from a prior, simpler setting carries over by analogy — the composite/operator-mixed object usually breaks the inherited proof and the gap must be closed independently.
4. Convert the spectral guarantee into the end-quantity the field rewards: a finite, computable threshold (lifting degree, factorization rank), a convergence rate decoupled from the condition number, a generalization or attribution bound, or a completeness claim that no further invariants exist. The condition under which the guarantee binds must be verifiable and non-vacuous; a bound resting on an unproven ansatz, or one that degrades under the curse of dimensionality or unestimable distributional structure, undermines the whole chain.
5. Validate that the substitution moves the needle against the strongest available baseline — the cheap surrogate matches the exact second-order method's quality, or the reshaped landscape delivers the claimed escape/convergence empirically. A correct theorem proven only in a contrived regime (width-1 layers, a single response-function form, infinite width with no finite-width discussion) or shipped without strong baselines reads as an artifact of the assumptions rather than a general advance.

## Differentiation within parent

Sibling cluster 12 also wields Hessian eigenvalue spectra, but it keeps an existing forward-pass module intact and uses curvature as a *diagnostic lens* to re-attribute that module's generalization to landscape flattening — curvature is the explanatory instrument, the module is unchanged. Cluster 30 instead makes the curvature/second-order object of the *optimization dynamics itself* the thing it substitutes, approximates, or reshapes, and its deliverable is an operational guarantee (saddle escape, conditioning-decoupled convergence, scalable attribution, conserved-quantity completeness) rather than a mechanistic re-attribution. It is likewise distinct from cluster 14, whose substituted object is a forward-pass representation (dual content/position vectors with disentangled interaction terms) in service of expressivity, not a second-order object in service of training-dynamics behavior. In short: 12 reads curvature to reinterpret a layer, 14 swaps the representation inside the forward map, and 30 substitutes or re-conditions the curvature operator that drives learning.

## When to pick this one

Choose this instantiation when your bottleneck lives in the second-order structure of the training dynamics — the Hessian/NTK/Fisher spectrum, ill-conditioning, spurious-minima or saddle geometry, or curvature-based scoring — rather than in a forward-pass operator's runtime or a representation's expressive capacity. The tell is that the quantity you need to control is a spectral property of how learning proceeds, not of what the network computes on a single input.

## Tactical failure mode

The dominant failure is substituting curvature for a recognized cheaper proxy — a fixed Hessian approximation, an incrementally-maintained sketch, a continuous-ODE reformulation, or a standard NTK-style preconditioner — and applying it to a new but narrow setting, so the work reads as an incremental re-skin of a known technique rather than a new spectral insight. A second, deeper failure is letting the spectral-preservation claim rest on an unproven ansatz or on a positive-definiteness result borrowed from a simpler regime that does not actually transfer to the composite/operator-mixed object; when the linchpin assumption is only empirically motivated, every downstream theorem collapses with it. A related shortcut is proving the curvature-decoupling or acceleration guarantee only in a contrived regime — width-1 layers, a single response-function form, infinite width with no finite-width bridge — which makes the result an artifact of the assumption. The recurring self-check: have I isolated *which* spectral quantity my substitute uniquely preserves, proven that preservation for *my* structure rather than by analogy, and shown the guarantee binds outside a toy regime against a strong baseline? If the surrogate is general but the proof is borrowed, or the proof is rigorous but the regime is contrived, the contribution will not clear the bar.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- A relaxation device used elsewhere only to build convex surrogates can be transplanted into a non-convex landscape to inject negative curvature at spurious stationary points, converting trapped minima into escapable strict saddles.
- Reducing an infinite-dimensional question — what is preserved across all descent trajectories? — to a finite Lie-algebraic computation lets you both enumerate the conserved quantities and certify that the list is complete.
- Treating a product parameterization as a jointly-optimized factored problem rather than a one-sided linear projection can collapse the rank needed for a no-spurious-minima guarantee by an order in the sample count.
- If a second-order criterion is only ever consumed as a ranking, proving it is exactly equivalent to a first-order outlier statistic in gradient space eliminates the curvature inversion entirely while keeping the guarantee.
- When practitioners declare a factored curvature approximation inapplicable to a heterogeneous architecture, the highest-yield move is often composing already-existing per-operator-type extensions, not inventing a new approximation.
- Recasting a structured hardware or algorithmic perturbation as an implicit, state-dependent regularizer rather than as noise makes a single unified correction provably valid across all shapes of the perturbation.

### Reject lessons
- Restructuring a known cheap curvature proxy into a drop-in first-order update reads as incremental unless you isolate and prove the specific spectral quantity it uniquely preserves.
- A spectral-equivalence ansatz that is empirically suggestive but formally unproven becomes a single point of failure that collapses every theorem built on top of it.
- Maintaining a curvature surrogate by incremental low-rank updates is not novel on its own, and a regret/convergence analysis that ignores the drift between the surrogate and the true low-rank object yields an unsound guarantee.
- Proving a conditioning-decoupling or depth-acceleration result only under a contrived regime (width-1 hidden layers, a single fixed response-function form) makes the advance read as an artifact of the restrictive assumption.
- Reformulating a discrete update rule as a continuous ODE solved by an adaptive integrator is a recognized equivalence; absent a strong baseline and a clearly removed bottleneck, it reads as a re-skin rather than a contribution.
- Extending an established NTK convergence template to a different optimizer without strong motivation or any in-submission experiments reads as a straightforward exercise, not a conceptual advance.
