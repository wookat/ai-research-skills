# C03
_parent: **Adapt by Conditioning, Not Retraining** (`adapt_via_conditioning`)_

## Tactical pattern

Express a whole family of heterogeneous target tasks in one canonical conditioning schema — an instruction/prompt template, a reference-query example layout, a retrieved (state, outcome, action) tuple sequence, a relational graph, or transferred per-class statistics — and then run a single upstream phase (broad multi-task supervised tuning, meta-learning over a procedurally generated task distribution, or synthetic-data pretraining) that teaches one frozen model to PARSE that schema and compose a never-seen task's answer from its conditioning inputs at inference. A complementary sub-move skips upstream tuning entirely and instead transfers the reference substrate itself into the query's prediction through a shared learned representation space: interpolating location/spread statistics from similar categories, retrieving nearest reference instances as a first-class input, aligning the target distribution to the source before adaptation, or amplifying a base-vs-adapted density-gradient offset. The load-bearing artifacts are the unified format and the reference substrate the model draws on, not new task-specific parameters. The generalization claim is earned by holding out entire task categories or target distributions and evaluating only there, rather than holding out instances of seen categories.

## Step-by-Step

1. Choose a single canonical conditioning schema that can express many heterogeneous target tasks as one input-output format, and make the schema carry the task specification implicitly so a never-seen task needs no new parameters. The format itself should be the thing the model learns to read. Verify the schema is more than a thin re-skin of an existing conditioning scheme, because reviewers read a cosmetic reformat as a domain port with no contribution.
2. Assemble the reference substrate the model will compose from: a broad multi-task corpus spanning many task categories, a procedurally generated or synthetic task distribution, or a retrievable bank of reference instances embedded in a shared representation space. Push for breadth across categories rather than depth on one, since a substrate covering a single task or one narrow primitive yields gains that look task-specific and unsurprising however large they are per task.
3. Run one upstream phase that teaches the model to interpret the conditioning format rather than memorize answers — multi-task tuning, meta-learning under a frontier curriculum that tracks the learner's current ability, or synthetic pretraining — or, when skipping upstream tuning, align the query directly to the reference substrate in a shared space (transfer statistics, retrieve neighbors, amplify a distributional offset). Keep 'how to read conditioning' decoupled from 'what the answer is'; collapsing the two quietly reintroduces per-task fitting.
4. Define the generalization test at the level of task categories or target distributions, not held-out instances: exclude entire clusters during the upstream phase and evaluate exclusively on the excluded ones. Audit for overlap between the upstream corpus (and any base-model pretraining) and the evaluation set, because an unaudited leak silently converts the held-out claim into ordinary in-distribution performance and draws a contamination rejection.
5. Isolate the contribution against the adversarial baseline a skeptic reaches for first — a simpler conditioning variant given the same budget, such as nearest-neighbor lookup, a longer raw context, multi-task tuning without your format, or retrieval without the new fusion module — and report exactly where your move wins and where it does not. Comparing only against ablations of your own method, or omitting the cheap alternative entirely, leaves the reported gain unattributable to the proposed mechanism.

## When to pick this one

Instantiate this tactic when you can both name a single format or shared representation space that expresses your entire target-task family and assemble a broad reference substrate (multi-task corpus, synthetic task distribution, or retrievable bank), so that generalization can be earned by holding out whole categories rather than tuning per task. The clearest trigger is a setting where a parameter-free or near-free conditioning baseline already rivals a much larger trained model, signalling an unexploited structural inductive bias.

## Tactical failure mode

The recurring failure shape is adopting a conditioning recipe known to work in one domain and porting it to a new modality or task family without any theory for why the conditioning signal carries task-relevant structure there, then reporting only modest gains over strong baselines. The headline 'training-free,' 'zero-shot,' or 'held-out generalization' claim is undercut because the experimental setup quietly violates its own premise — evaluation data or near-duplicates leaked into the upstream or base-model pretraining corpus, the method needs idealized access (the exact base checkpoint), or the demonstrated scope is a single task with no recipe for finding the analog elsewhere. These papers also omit the one baseline that would isolate the contribution — a simpler conditioning variant (nearest-neighbor, longer context, multi-task tuning without the format) under equal budget — and instead compare against ablations of their own method, leaving the gain unattributable; multi-component variants compound this with ablations too shallow to justify each module. Self-check: can you name a simpler conditioning baseline you beat under matched budget, prove no leakage between your upstream corpus and your held-out categories, and show the move on more than one task or modality?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- When labels are sparse, fix the estimated distribution rather than the classifier: transferring spread statistics from structurally similar well-sampled categories is near-free and beats adding model capacity.
- Teaching a model to parse one unified task format across many task categories makes the format itself the learned skill, enabling transfer to structurally novel held-out categories rather than overfitting to seen task types.
- Reframe a cross-domain gap as distributional rather than structural: aligning representation distributions before adaptation reuses accumulated pretrained knowledge and removes the need for domain-specific architectural redesign.
- In-context adaptation can be made to emerge by jointly scaling model capacity, memory horizon, and task-distribution breadth under a curriculum that tracks the learner's frontier — generalization is a joint-scaling problem, not an architecture-alone problem.
- Train a context-interpreter on cheap procedurally generated synthetic data so parameters encode 'how to read context' instead of 'what to do,' decoupling the capability from collecting target-task data.
- A parameter-free nearest-instance baseline that already matches large models exposes retrieval as an unexploited structural inductive bias — make retrieval a first-class trained input modality, not a post-hoc augmentation.

### Reject lessons
- Claiming 'fine-tuning-free' or 'zero-shot' while training splits of the evaluation tasks sit inside your upstream corpus makes the comparison unfair and collapses the generalization claim into in-distribution performance.
- A conditioning trick shown on a single task, with no generic procedure for finding its analog on other tasks, reads as narrow and unsurprising however large the per-task gain.
- Porting an established conditioning technique to a new modality without theory for why its similarity signal captures task-relevant structure, paired with only modest gains, reads as an under-theorized transfer that does not clear the bar.
- Omitting the obvious cheaper alternative under equal budget — a longer raw context, nearest-neighbor averaging — and comparing only against ablations of your own method leaves the new module's contribution unattributable.
- A method that works only under idealized access (the exact base model) and degrades sharply at realistic scale or under the dominant parameter-efficient setting fails the generality bar.
- A many-component conditioning system with shallow ablations, unclear problem formulation, and uncorrected mathematical details cannot justify which module earns the reported improvement.
