# C13
_parent: **Encode Structure by Construction** (`structural_prior_encoding`)_

## Tactical pattern

This cluster's move is to make the relational connectivity topology itself the encoded structural prior: define which entities link to which (and under what relation type), then run message-passing/propagation over that wiring so a specific structural quantity is captured by construction rather than relearned by a generic dense or attention model. Concretely the papers either (a) construct the edge set from geometry or features — project points onto a sphere and take the convex hull to fix edges, reweight edges by a feature-proximity kernel before local diffusion, build BFS dependency networks from a domain schema; (b) match the aggregation operator to each relation's topology — cyclic message-passing for feedback loops, set-membership/hypergraph aggregation for overlapping groups, subspace-decoupled Hodge operators, scalar compatibility potentials over output sequences; or (c) approximate an expensive structural statistic — shared-neighbor counts, local overlap — via propagated compact sketches or cached neighbor histories. The load-bearing object is always the graph/relation wiring or a statistic over it, not a transformation-group symmetry.

## Step-by-Step

1. Name the exact relational object your task depends on — a connectivity pattern, a structural statistic (shared-neighbor counts, local overlap), a relation-typed topology, or a geometric invariant — and state precisely what a generic dense/attention model would otherwise have to relearn. Boundary: if you can only say 'the graph structure' generically rather than a named quantity, you will read as an incremental graph-construction paper.
2. Construct the topology or the operator so that the target object is satisfied or captured by construction: derive edges from geometry or feature proximity, read connectivity from a known schema while assigning each relation type an operator whose inductive bias matches its semantics, or replace a generic operator with a structurally-matched one. Boundary: favor a parameter-free or principled construction over a tunable add-on module — bolting an extra learned block onto an existing pipeline reads as assembly, not contribution.
3. Isolate the load-bearing step and demonstrate via ablation what breaks without it: removing it should collapse the model either to a generic baseline (losing the structural property) or to an intractable one (losing efficiency). Boundary: if deleting your construction leaves performance roughly unchanged, the structure was never load-bearing and the framing is hollow.
4. Attach a guarantee that the construction genuinely captures the claimed structure — an expressiveness proof, a recovery/identifiability result, a combinatorial bound, or an invariance certificate — and pair it with an efficiency or scalability argument. Boundary: a domain-motivated architecture with no guarantee that its wiring captures the claimed structure, and no statistical or expressiveness backing, is the single most common failure here.
5. Validate against the simplest structure-agnostic explanation of the gains (correlation heuristics, a hand-crafted structural-proximity score, a single-stream model) under a realistic, non-inflated evaluation protocol. Boundary: balanced or easy evaluation sets and absent simple baselines routinely hide that a hand-crafted heuristic already matches your construction, which dissolves the motivation for the added structure.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster is distinct from sibling cluster 16, which also 'encodes structure by construction' but whose structural object is a transformation/symmetry group: cluster 16 makes a base procedure invariant by averaging it over a small representative frame of group transformations (exact symmetrization made cheap), so its move is about group actions on the whole input. Cluster 13's structural object is instead the relational connectivity topology — who connects to whom and under which relation type — and its move is to construct or refine the graph wiring (or the relation-typed operator over it) so a structural statistic or geometric property holds by construction. It is also unlike cluster 20, which targets robustness of a corrupted learner via internal salience alignment rather than encoding any relational topology at all. Where 16 asks 'what transformation leaves the answer unchanged', cluster 13 asks 'what is the connectivity graph and what statistic over it must be preserved'.

## When to pick this one

Pick this cluster when your known structure is a relational/connectivity topology — entities joined by typed relations, geometry-induced adjacency, or a domain relational schema — or a named statistic over such a graph, rather than a symmetry group acting on the input. The trigger is that your prior knowledge takes the form 'who is connected to whom and how' (or 'this structural count matters'), not 'the output is invariant to this transformation'.

## Tactical failure mode

The recurring reject shape (drawn from a healthy 12-paper Reject sample) is assembling existing relational-learning components — message passing plus attention plus sequential state tracking, or a domain-derived graph plus a standard GNN — into a problem-specific architecture, then claiming the encoded relational structure as the contribution without proving it. The wrong assumption is that wiring domain or relational structure into a model is itself the advance: reviewers instead read incremental assembly whenever the genuinely novel piece versus the inherited components is not sharply delineated. The construction typically carries no guarantee (no expressiveness, recovery, identifiability, or uniqueness result) that its wiring actually captures the claimed structural property. Compounding this, evaluation uses inflated or balanced protocols and omits the simplest structure-agnostic comparison, so it stays invisible that a hand-crafted heuristic or correlation baseline already suffices. A candidate can self-check by asking: can I name the exact statistic captured, prove the construction captures it, show via ablation that removing it collapses the model, and beat the cheapest baseline on a realistic distribution — if any answer is no, this is the failure mode.

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- Separate the structural-information requirement from the expensive mechanism that conventionally supplies it: if a statistic like shared-neighbor counts can be approximated by a cheap propagated summary, you capture expressiveness and efficiency in one move.
- A parameter-free construction that simultaneously certifies several previously-competing properties — sparsity, global connectivity, and geometric rigidity via a classical combinatorial bound — beats a tunable module that secures only one.
- Reframe relations as constraints over the output space (scalar compatibility functions) rather than as forward generators, which buys compositionality, test-time editability, and anomaly scoring for free.
- Inject auxiliary signal such as feature proximity into the edge weights of a purely local procedure so it becomes feature-aware at no loss of locality, and back the construction with a statistical recovery guarantee.
- Aim for the rare triple contribution — a theorem (expressiveness or recovery), an efficiency argument, and SOTA results — since pairing the structural construction with proof plus speed is what separates accepts from plausible-looking architectures.
- Treat the structure's own connectivity as a free supervisory signal: the absence of a link is as informative as its presence and can directly supervise the relational weighting.

### Reject lessons
- Combining borrowed modules (global plus local streams, attention plus state tracking) with no genuinely new mechanism reads as incremental even when the combination is sensible.
- A domain-grounded architecture lacking any theoretical guarantee that its wiring captures the claimed structure — and lacking a uniqueness or identifiability argument — leaves reviewers unable to credit the structural claim.
- Inheriting the core operator and its constraints from prior work and merely porting them to a new domain collapses the contribution to assembly unless the incremental piece is delineated explicitly.
- Inflated or balanced evaluation protocols can mask that a hand-crafted structural heuristic already outperforms the learned construction in the realistic distribution, removing the motivation for added complexity.
- Omitting closely-related SOTA baselines and the simplest correlation or heuristic comparison makes any claimed structural advantage unverifiable.
- Proposing a new construction or task without analyzing the information loss it introduces (compressing histories into profiles, projecting away radial distance) invites doubt about whether the encoded structure helps or hurts.
