# C29
_parent: **Audit and Pivot an Assumption** (`assumption_audit_and_pivot`)_

## Tactical pattern

The shared move is to take an existing convergence or regret guarantee for an iterative first-order/adaptive update procedure (gradient descent, per-coordinate adaptive methods, Polyak/clipped/shuffling variants, interacting-particle or policy-gradient updates) and pivot the single regularity-or-target assumption the proof silently rests on. Concretely, authors either swap a global Lipschitz-smoothness assumption for a position- or trajectory-local one (an (L0,L1)-type or local rate-of-change bound), or replace the convergence primitive itself — exact-optimum target → a characterized neighborhood, squared-distance-to-solution measure → a direct objective-gap, metric approximation error → ordinal rank-preservation, boundedness of raw update signals → boundedness of signal residuals, mean-field limit → joint N-particle divergence. They then re-prove the rate by isolating one load-bearing object (a per-iteration progress bound, a fixed-point-preserving identity, or a cumulative-deficit decomposition) that all corollaries instantiate. The payoff is a sharper rate, a provable separation between method classes, or coverage of a nonsmooth/constrained/heavy-tailed/finite-particle regime the original assumption excluded.

## Step-by-Step

1. Pin down the single regularity or target-criterion assumption the existing convergence proof for the procedure relies on, and argue it is not a neutral simplification but the exact thing foreclosing the result you want — a faster rate, a separation between method classes, or applicability to a harder regime. The diagnosis must attribute the persistent theory–practice gap to the assumption itself, not to insufficient proof sophistication. Importing an assumption from a neighboring literature without showing it is the load-bearing obstacle in your own setting invites the 'borrowed assumption, benefits uninvestigated' dismissal.
2. Replace that assumption with the weakest condition that still admits a clean proof: either a position/trajectory-local version of the regularity, or an entirely different convergence primitive — a neighborhood instead of the exact optimum, a direct objective-gap instead of geometric proximity, an ordinal/rank condition instead of metric error, residual-of-signal instead of raw signal, or a lifted joint object instead of a limiting approximation. The replacement must be verifiable and exhibited on concrete function or problem classes; a condition no one can check is read as reverse-engineered to make the proof close.
3. Re-derive the rate from scratch under the new condition, and isolate the one load-bearing inequality, identity, or decomposition that every corollary instantiates — a single per-iteration progress bound, a fixed-point-preserving substitution, or a split of the rate-controlling quantity into a universally-bounded part and an assumption-sensitive part. Confirm the bound stays tight and that no hidden constant in the big-O explodes at the parameter limit where you claim the result is strongest.
4. Prove the new condition strictly buys something the old one could not: a separation showing one method class converges where another provably diverges, a faster rate than the prior bound, or coverage of a nonsmooth, constrained, heavy-tailed, or finite-sample regime the original assumption silently excluded. A relaxation that yields only the baseline rate with cosmetic algorithmic changes will be read as an incremental extension over the closest existing method, however elegant the proof.
5. Anchor the abstract guarantee to the motivating practice: name at least one concrete setting where the weakened condition demonstrably holds and where the procedure's advantage is observable, rather than stopping at the theorem. A single toy instance or a narrow benchmark-only validation leaves reviewers unable to judge whether the relaxed assumption captures a real advance, and a theorem stated without formal assumptions is treated as not-yet-a-paper.

## Differentiation within parent

This cluster pivots the assumption of a *convergence/regret guarantee for an iterative optimization dynamics* and keeps the procedure essentially fixed, changing only the analytical primitive (regularity condition, convergence target, or measurement criterion) to re-prove a rate. Sibling cluster 19 also lives in the optimization/learning-theory space but its move is to *modify the operator itself* — replacing a hard truncation with a smooth dampening — and then derive a distribution-free *generalization* bound that it optimizes as the training objective; the target is statistical (generalization) and the algorithm is altered, whereas here the target is dynamics (rate/separation) and the algorithm is held constant. It is likewise distinct from sibling cluster 28, whose move is architectural decoupling of distributed optimization into a local update and an aggregation-side optimizer rather than auditing a load-bearing assumption of an existing proof.

## When to pick this one

Pick this instantiation when the artifact whose assumption you are auditing is a convergence or regret guarantee for an iterative update procedure, the load-bearing assumption is a regularity condition or a convergence-target/measurement criterion, and the payoff you seek is a rate, a class separation, or coverage of a harder regime — not a generalization bound or a new architectural mechanism.

## Tactical failure mode

The dominant failure shape is a relaxed assumption that is technically valid but uncharacterized: no named function or problem class is shown to satisfy it, and the behavior of its constants as dimension or iteration grows is unstudied, so reviewers read it as engineered to make the proof close rather than as a genuine generalization. A second recurring shape is a re-derived rate that merely matches the existing baseline with near-identical algorithmic changes, which lands as an incremental extension over the closest prior method regardless of the proof's elegance. A third is a headline guarantee undermined by hidden constants in the big-O that explode at the very parameter limit where the result is claimed strongest — a defect reviewers find by reading the proof, not the statement. Compounding these, the empirical anchor is often a single toy instance or a narrow benchmark that fails to validate the motivating application, or the paper states 'tight, assumption-free' results with no exhibited assumptions or precise theorems at all. Self-check: can you name a concrete class satisfying your weakened condition, does the relaxation buy a rate or separation the old assumption provably could not, and do your constants stay finite at the regime you advertise?

## Examples
### Oral lessons
- The highest-value assumption to attack is not the one that simplifies the proof but the one that silently erases the exact regime where your method's advantage would appear — relax that and a separation between method classes falls out.
- Switching the convergence target from the exact optimum to a neighborhood whose radius you explicitly characterize can unlock a strictly faster rate while staying compatible with mechanisms that exact convergence forbids.
- When a proof routes through a proxy quantity that breaks in your target regime, bounding the quantity you actually care about directly is often the only unblock — the proxy was the obstacle, not the difficulty.
- Lifting the object of analysis to a higher-dimensional or joint representation can expose a tractable dominant signal that the lower-dimensional framing hid, letting you bypass a lossy intermediate approximation entirely.
- Recasting a magnitude bound on raw update signals as the same bound on signal differences restores the fixed-point property at the solution, so the relaxed analysis still converges to the exact target instead of a biased one.
- Decomposing a cumulative performance measure into one universally-bounded component and one assumption-sensitive component reduces the entire characterization to bounding a single tractable term and can reveal a sharp threshold.

### Reject lessons
- A weakened assumption that no known function class is shown to satisfy reads as engineered to close the proof, not as a genuine generalization — characterize when it holds or it sinks the paper.
- Borrowing a relaxed condition from a neighboring literature without demonstrating its concrete benefit in your own setting leaves reviewers asking what the assumption actually buys.
- Re-deriving a rate that merely matches the baseline with near-identical algorithmic changes is read as an incremental extension, however clean the analysis.
- Hidden constants that explode at the precise parameter limit where the result is advertised as strongest silently invalidate the headline guarantee, and reviewers find them in the proof.
- A theorem stated without formal assumptions or a precise claim is unreviewable; 'tight, assumption-free' bounds with no exhibited assumptions are treated as not-yet-a-paper.
- An assumption-relaxation validated only on a single toy instance gives no evidence the relaxation captures the motivating practical regime.
